## Step-by-Step: Basic Nail Polish Application

### Step 1: Prep and Clean

1. Remove all old polish with acetone remover
2. Trim nails to your desired length
3. File to shape (square, round, oval, or almond — choose one and commit)
4. Push back cuticles using the pusher — do not cut them (cutting risks infection)
5. Wipe each nail with an alcohol pad or nail cleanser to remove oils

> **Beginner key**: Clean prep = polish that actually sticks. Skip this and your manicure will chip in days.

### Step 2: Base Coat

Apply a thin, even layer of base coat to all nails. Brush from center outward to the sides, and make sure to cover the full nail surface including the very edge (this "tip sealing" is crucial for longevity).

**Why base coat matters:**
- Prevents color staining on your natural nail
- Creates a surface that color polish bonds to
- Significantly extends how long your manicure lasts

### Step 3: Color Application (Two Coats)

**First coat**: Thin, even layer. Don't worry if it's slightly patchy — this is just the foundation.
**Wait 2 minutes for first coat to dry.**
**Second coat**: Even, slightly more generous layer to build full opacity.

Polish technique tips:
- Start at the center of the nail base, pull toward the tip in one stroke
- Use thin coats — thin wins over thick every time
- "Wrap the tips" — lightly stroke the very edge of the nail to seal it

### Step 4: Top Coat

Apply a quick-dry top coat over all nails, wrapping the tips again. Top coat determines the final finish (glossy, matte, etc.) and dramatically increases chip resistance.

### Step 5: Clean-Up

Use a thin brush or pointed cotton swab dipped in remover to clean any polish that got on your skin. Clean edges make even simple manicures look professional.

### Prep (Same as above)

### Gel Application Steps

1. **Buff nail surface**: Lightly buff the nail with 180-grit file to improve gel adhesion
2. **Cleanse**: Wipe with alcohol pad
3. **Gel base coat**: Thin layer, cure under LED lamp for 60 seconds
4. **Gel color coat 1**: Thin layer, cure 60 seconds
5. **Gel color coat 2**: Even coverage layer, cure 60 seconds
6. **Gel top coat**: Even layer, cure 60–120 seconds (check product instructions)
7. **Wipe inhibition layer**: Clean the tacky surface with alcohol pad (some no-wipe top coats skip this)

**Key gel rules:**
- Each layer MUST fully cure before the next — never rush the lamp time
- Thin coats always — thick gel lifts and bubbles
- After top coat, avoid getting nails wet for at least 1 hour

## Common Beginner Mistakes and Fixes

### Mistake 1: Polish peels off within days

**Why**: Oils left on the nail surface, skipping base coat, not wrapping tips
**Fix**: Alcohol-wipe before every application, always use base coat, seal every nail edge

### Mistake 2: Bubbles in the polish

**Why**: Painting too fast, shaking the bottle, thick coats
**Fix**: Roll the bottle gently (don't shake), apply in thin layers, let each coat dry fully

### Mistake 3: Polish scratches and chips easily

**Why**: No top coat, or a low-quality top coat
**Fix**: Invest in a quality quick-dry top coat (Seche Vite or similar quick-dry formulas are worth it)

### Mistake 4: Gel stays soft after curing

**Why**: Underpowered lamp, not enough cure time, coats too thick
**Fix**: Use a 36W+ LED lamp, cure each layer for the full recommended time, apply thin coats

### Mistake 5: Gel lifting at the edges

**Why**: Gel base coat touching the surrounding skin, breaking adhesion
**Fix**: Leave a 0.5mm gap between base coat and skin — never let gel touch your cuticle or skin

## Best Starter Styles for Beginners (Easiest to Harder)

1. **Solid color** — master application technique first, nothing else required
2. **French tips with guide strips** — adhesive guides make this achievable for anyone
3. **Ombre / gradient (sponge method)** — sponge-dabbing two colors at the boundary creates a blend without brushwork
4. **Dot designs (dotting tool)** — a toothpick or dotting tool makes polka dots and simple patterns accessible
5. **Line art** — a thin detail brush for simple geometric lines; minimal skill, strong impact
