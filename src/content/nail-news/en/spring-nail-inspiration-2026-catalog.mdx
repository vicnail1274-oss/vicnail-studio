## Spring 2026 Nail Inspiration Catalog: 30+ Designs Across 5 Color Families

Spring 2026 brings the most diverse range of nail trends in recent memory. From the quiet luxury of soap nails to the artistic complexity of jelly chrome, there's a direction for every client aesthetic.

This catalog brings together 30+ designs across the five dominant color families of spring 2026, organized by what clients are actually booking.

---

## The Five Color Families of Spring 2026

### 1. Milky & Nude Family

The defining aesthetic of spring 2026. These shades look expensive, clean, and appropriate for every occasion.

**Design 1: Classic Milky White**
- Single color, glossy finish
- Shape: squoval or rounded
- Accent: thin gold French tip
- Best for: professional environments, brides

**Design 2: Nude Pink Gradient**
- 2-3 shade gradient from cuticle to free edge
- Matte top coat
- Shape: almond or oval
- Best for: soft, romantic aesthetic

**Design 3: Pearl Double French**
- Milky white base
- Second French line in pale pink, 1mm thick
- Slight shimmer in top coat
- Best for: clients wanting subtle sophistication

---

### 2. Coral & Warm Family

The surprise climber of 2026. Warmer than last year's peach, coral reads as sophisticated rather than playful.

**Design 4: Solid Coral Glossy**
- Single rich coral, 2 coats, glossy finish
- Shape: rounded square
- Best for: clients wanting warmth without boldness

**Design 5: Coral-to-Orange Ombre**
- Gradient from coral at cuticle to soft orange at free edge
- Matte finish
- Shape: coffin
- Best for: creative clients, summer-forward bookings

**Design 6: Terracotta French**
- Terracotta base
- Thin cream-white French tip
- Minimalist nail art
- Best for: mature clients, professional warm aesthetic

---

### 3. Lavender & Purple Family

The artistic choice. Purple has staying power — it's not just a seasonal trend, it's an established year-round option that gets more saturated in spring.

**Design 7: Pale Lavender Milky**
- 2 coats pale lavender jelly
- Glossy finish
- Shape: rounded
- Best for: universal appeal, beginners to nail art

**Design 8: Lavender Chrome Accent**
- Matte lavender base
- One accent nail (index) in silver chrome
- Shape: almond
- Best for: clients wanting artistic edge

**Design 9: Gradient Lavender Set**
- 5 nail gradient from deep violet cuticle to pale lilac free edge
- Glossy top coat
- Best for: artistic clients, fashion-forward

---

### 4. Blue & Cool Family

The calm alternative to warm tones. Powder blue and periwinkle are the cool-toned choices that photograph beautifully.

**Design 10: Powder Blue Solid**
- 2 coats powder blue
- Matte or satin finish
- Shape: squoval
- Best for: everyday elegance

**Design 11: Ocean Glass**
- Clear jelly base with blue micro-glitter suspended
- 3 coats for depth
- Shape: rounded
- Best for: vacation-ready clients

**Design 12: French Tip Blue**
- Clear/nude base
- Powder blue French tip (not white — blue)
- Thin line, modern French interpretation
- Best for: clients wanting updated classic

---

### 5. Green & Earthy Family

The unexpected trend. Sage, olive, and forest green are all trending upward as clients seek nature-inspired palettes.

**Design 13: Sage Green Solid**
- 2 coats sage green
- Glossy finish
- Shape: rounded square
- Best for: clients wanting understated elegance

**Design 14: Olive & Gold Accent**
- Olive green base
- One gold accent nail
- Minimalist line art or dot
- Shape: almond
- Best for: fashion-conscious clients

**Design 15: Mint Glass**
- Clear jelly base with green micro-shimmer
- 3 coats for opacity
- Shape: stiletto
- Best for: bold clients, special occasions

---

## The Universal Designs (Work for Every Client)

**Design 16: The Clean French**
- Milky white base
- Extra-thin white French tip (1-2mm)
- Soft rounded shape
- Glossy top coat
- Why it works: universally flattering, professional, grows out gracefully

**Design 17: The One-Color Glow**
- Single favorite shade (any from this catalog)
- Glossy finish
- Simple shape (rounded or squoval)
- Why it works: clients who know what they want

**Design 18: The Accent Nail**
- 4 nails: single favorite color, glossy finish
- 1 nail: accent (gold dot, small crystal, glitter gradient)
- Why it works: adds interest without commitment

## The Bottom Line

Spring 2026 offers more diversity than any recent season. The five color families — milky/nude, coral/warm, lavender/purple, blue/cool, green/earthy — give every client a direction that works.

The universal designs (16-18) are your workhorse. Stock the milky/nude palette as your base, then add accent options based on client aesthetic.

Book spring clients for milky white and soft coral. Upsell with one accent nail or a gradient. The rest takes care of itself.
