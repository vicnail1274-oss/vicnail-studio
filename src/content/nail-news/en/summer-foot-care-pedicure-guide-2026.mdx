## Step 1: Exfoliation — The Foundation of Foot Care

The heels, the sides of the feet, and toe knuckles are where calluses accumulate most heavily. If you skip exfoliation, even the best moisturizer won't penetrate properly.

### DIY Exfoliation Methods

**Soak and Soften (gentlest option)**
1. Add a little bath salt or baking soda to warm water and soak for 10–15 minutes
2. Use a pumice stone or foot file to gently buff heels and foot sides in circular motions
3. Towel dry and immediately apply moisturizer while skin is still slightly damp

**Urea Foot Mask (strongest effect, best for lazy days)**
- A foot mask containing 20–40% urea (easily found at Taiwanese drugstores under "hard skin removal foot mask")
- Put on the included plastic booties and wait 60–90 minutes
- Dead skin peels off naturally about a week later — no scrubbing required

**Important notes:**
- People with diabetes or foot wounds should avoid using sharp tools on foot skin; see a medical professional instead
- Over-buffing thins the skin and actually accelerates callus buildup — once every 2–3 weeks is sufficient

## Step 2: Toenail Trimming and Shaping

Toenails follow different rules from fingernails. While fingernails can be shaped in many ways, there's one critical principle for toenails:

**Trim toenails straight across (square shape).** Never round the corners — especially leave the side corners intact to prevent ingrown toenails (onychocryptosis).

### Trimming Steps

1. Trim after soaking or showering when nails are softened — much easier
2. Use wide-jaw toenail clippers (wider than fingernail clippers) to cut straight across
3. Lightly file edges with a nail file so corners don't catch on skin
4. Push back cuticles with an orange stick (never cut cuticles with scissors!)

### Toenail Health Self-Check

- **Healthy toenails**: Light pink, smooth surface, normal thickness
- **Watch for**: Yellow or thickened nails (possible fungal infection), nails digging into skin (ingrown), white spots on nail surface (usually minor impact, not a health concern)

If you suspect a fungal nail infection, see a doctor. Home nail care cannot treat it.

## Step 3: Deep Moisturizing — From Heel to Toe

Foot skin has no sebaceous glands, making it naturally drier than hand skin. Summer makes it worse — wearing sandals and going barefoot accelerates moisture loss.

### Daily Moisturizing Routine

**Pre-sleep foot care (5 minutes)**
1. After bathing, while feet still have some moisture, immediately apply your moisturizer
2. Focus on: heels, inner ankles, and between the toes (all prone to dryness and cracking)
3. Wear thin cotton socks to sleep — this "sleeping foot mask" method dramatically improves absorption

### Recommended Product Types

| Product | Best for | Frequency |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Urea cream (10%) | General dryness | Daily |
| Urea cream (25%+) | Severe cracking | Daily, morning and night |
| Foot oil (shea butter) | Crack repair | Nightly |
| Mint cooling foot cream | Summer swelling | Summer evenings |

## Step 4: Summer Toenail Color Guide

Pedicure (toe gel or nail polish) lasts longer than hand manicure — typically 4–6 weeks. The key to summer toenail color is "vivid, saturated, presence" — because toenails are viewed from a distance, pale colors tend to disappear visually.

### Top 2026 Summer Toenail Colors

**Coral Red Family**
- Representative shades: tomato red, red-orange, coral orange
- Why recommended: pairs perfectly with flip-flops and sandals for lively visual energy
- Best for: neutral to tan skin tones

**Earth-Tone Darks**
- Representative shades: caramel brown, brick red, terracotta orange
- Why recommended: long-lasting visual appeal, doesn't show wear or dirt, great for everyday wear
- Best for: deeper skin tones where they really pop

**Bold Brights**
- Representative shades: electric blue, neon yellow, mango yellow
- Why recommended: beach vacation energy, perfect for photos
- Best for: travel, parties

**French Pedicure**
- How: nude base + white tips; French on toes reads even more resort-ready than on hands
- 2026 upgrade: colored French tips (pink, blue, orange) for extra summer personality

### Nail Polish vs. Toe Gel: Which to Choose?

| Application | DIY-friendly | Nail artist recommended |

## Step 5: Daily Maintenance to Extend Your Pedicure

- **Minimize prolonged soaking**: After hot springs or swimming, gel can lift at the edges. Applying a thin coat of petroleum jelly over the nails before water activities provides some protection
- **Choose sandals with arch support**: Flat-sole flip-flops accelerate callus buildup with prolonged walking
- **Don't forget sunscreen on your feet**: With open-toe shoes, the top of the foot and toe gaps are prone to sunburn — apply SPF to the foot dorsum and between toes
