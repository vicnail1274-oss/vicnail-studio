## What Are Bubble Nails?

Bubble nails are a 3D nail art technique that creates **round, raised domes** on the surface of the nail. These raised shapes resemble soft air bubbles or slowly expanding candies — visually rhythmic, tactile, and impossible to miss.

Technically, bubble nails are typically built using **builder gel** or hard extension gel, which can be sculpted into dimensional shapes and cured under an LED/UV lamp. The technique requires considerable skill, which is why most bubble nail services are still best handled at a professional salon.

## Types of Bubble Nails

### 1. Classic Bubble (Single Dome)

The foundational style: one large, centered dome on each nail — like an oversized drop of water frozen mid-surface. Most commonly paired with solid neutral colors (nudes, whites) to let the 3D shape speak for itself.

### 2. Multi-Bubble

Two to five smaller domes arranged on a single nail, either in a neat row or scattered casually. The effect resembles pearls or bubble-gum candy — sweet, playful, and charming.

### 3. Petal / Floral Bubble

Multiple small domes arranged in the shape of a flower, often with a tiny rhinestone or bead at the center. Fresh and romantic, this style is especially popular for spring and summer looks.

### 4. Geometric Dimensional Nails

Instead of round shapes, this variation uses **diamond, square, or triangular** raised forms for a futuristic, architectural aesthetic. It's the go-to for avant-garde fashion lovers.

### 5. Mixed-Texture Bubble Nails

Combining the raised dome structure with **chrome powder, cat eye gel, or jelly gel** creates a nail that has both 3D dimension and luxe surface shimmer — one of the most popular salon combinations in 2026.

## How Are Bubble Nails Made?

### Tools Required
- Builder gel / hard gel / acrylic
- Detail sculpting brush
- LED/UV lamp
- Base coat, top coat
- Optional: rhinestones, chrome powder

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Nail prep**: Clean, push cuticles, lightly buff nail surface, apply base coat
2. **Plan placement**: Map out where the domes will be placed
3. **Build the dome**: Using the sculpting brush, pick up a small bead of hard gel and deposit it at the target location, letting it settle into a natural round dome shape
4. **Shape refinement**: While the gel is still uncured, gently refine the edges with the brush tip
5. **Cure**: Once satisfied with the shape, cure fully under the lamp
6. **Sand edges**: Use a fine grit nail file to smooth any uneven borders around the dome
7. **Apply color**: Paint over the full nail including the dome surface, ensuring even coverage on the curved area
8. **Finish**: Apply top coat and cure fully

> Bubble nails require advanced gel building skills. If trying at home, practice on nail tips before attempting on natural nails.

## Bubble Nail Care Tips

1. **Domes are fragile to direct impact**: Typing and household chores require extra care to avoid hitting the raised areas
2. **Wear gloves for cleaning**: Extend wear time by protecting nails during dishes and cleaning
3. **Regular salon check-ins**: Edges of the dome can wear down or develop micro-cracks — check in every 2 weeks
4. **Professional removal only**: Builder gel domes cannot be peeled off; they must be filed down or soaked off by a professional
