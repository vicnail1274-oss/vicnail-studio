Gel nails that lift before the two-week mark are one of the most frustrating experiences in nail care.

But here's the thing: early chipping and peeling usually isn't a skill issue — it's a maintenance issue. Here are **5 core tips that will help most people get 3+ weeks out of their gel manicure**, with habits that are easy to build into your routine.

---

## Tip 1: The First 48 Hours Are Critical — Minimize Extended Water Exposure

The two days after a gel application are when the bond between gel and nail plate is at its most vulnerable. Prolonged soaking (long baths, hand-washing dishes, swimming) during this window can cause the gel edges to lift prematurely.

**What to do:**
- For the first 48 hours, wear gloves when washing dishes or cleaning
- Opt for showers over baths, or keep your hands above water during baths
- After swimming, rinse your hands thoroughly and apply cuticle oil

---

## Tip 2: Daily Cuticle Oil — The Single Most Impactful Habit

Skipping aftercare entirely is the most common mistake gel nail wearers make. Dry cuticles cause the gel edges to crack and lift much faster than properly moisturized skin.

**What to do:**
- Apply **cuticle oil** around the nail edge daily — morning and before bed
- After washing hands, always follow up with hand cream
- Best ingredients to look for: jojoba oil, sweet almond oil, vitamin E

**Quick tip:** Drop cuticle oil directly onto the cuticle and massage for 30 seconds. No need to rinse.

---

## Tip 3: Stop Using Your Nails as Tools

Prying open packages, picking at stickers, or hammering on a keyboard with your fingertips — these habits are the number one cause of gel nail damage. They create shearing force at the tip that gradually detaches the gel from the nail plate.

**Habits to change:**
- Opening jars or zippers: use the pad of your finger, not the tip
- Tearing tape or packaging: use scissors or a blade
- Typing: adjust your posture so you're pressing with finger pads, not tapping with nail tips

Gel is more flexible than it looks — the damage usually comes from lateral shearing, not downward pressure.

---

## Tip 4: Use a Fine Nail File to Smooth Lifted Edges Before They Get Worse

If you notice a small lifted edge, **don't pick at it**. Picking removes a small piece and takes a large patch with it.

The right move: use a **180–240 grit nail file (the fine side of a buffer)** and very gently smooth down the lifted edge. Ten seconds of light filing can buy your gel manicure several more days before its next appointment.

**What to buy:** Any standard fine nail buffer from a beauty supply store will do — use the softest side.

---

## Tip 5: The Best Maintenance Is What Happens Before the Gel Goes On

The most impactful thing you can do for gel longevity isn't aftercare — it's proper prep:

- **Make sure your nail plate is oil-free:** Most nail technicians will clean the nails with alcohol before applying gel; do the same if touching up at home
- **Avoid moisturizer on hands before your appointment:** Don't apply hand lotion in the 30 minutes before a gel application
- **Tell your nail tech about your lifestyle:** If your hands are frequently in water, ask for an extra layer of base coat — it makes a noticeable difference in longevity

| After every hand wash | Apply hand cream |

---

## The Bottom Line

How long gel nails last is 70% technique and 30% aftercare. With the right daily habits, extending your gel manicure's life isn't complicated.

The three things that matter most: **use cuticle oil daily, avoid extended water exposure, and stop using your nails as tools**. Get those right, and you're already ahead of the curve.
