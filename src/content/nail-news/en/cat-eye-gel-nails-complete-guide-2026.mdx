Among all nail techniques, **cat eye gel** is one of the fastest-growing trends in recent years. Its signature look — a glowing band of light that shifts and flows across the nail like a cat's iris — creates a hypnotic, almost three-dimensional effect. In 2026, cat eye gel isn't fading out; it's leveling up with more varieties, more colors, and more creative combinations, making it one of the most-requested techniques at nail salons.

## What Is Cat Eye Gel?

Cat eye gel is a type of gel polish infused with **magnetic particles**. When the gel is applied and before it fully cures, a magnet wand is held close to the nail surface. The magnetic particles align themselves under the magnet's influence, forming a luminous "stripe" — the signature cat eye effect. Once cured under an LED or UV lamp, this stripe is locked in permanently, giving nails a striking depth and shimmer.

Since cat eye gel is a gel polish at its core, it requires LED/UV curing and typically lasts 2–3 weeks without chipping.

Aurora cat eye layers **multi-tone prismatic shimmer** over the traditional band, so the light stripe shifts through multiple colors depending on the viewing angle. ### 4. Pearl Cat Eye

Instead of metallic shimmer, the light band glows with a **soft pearl luster**. The result is feminine and gentle — appropriate for almost any occasion, and particularly beautiful for weddings and romantic settings.

### 5. Cat Eye + Chrome (Mirror Finish)

Combining cat eye gel with chrome powder creates an ultra-intense metallic mirror effect. This hybrid technique exploded in popularity across Korean nail circles in 2026 and is now spreading globally.

## How to Apply Cat Eye Gel: Step-by-Step

Cat eye gel requires some technique to execute well. Here's the standard process:

### Tools You'll Need
- Cat eye gel color (any type)
- LED/UV lamp (48W or higher recommended)
- Magnet wand — different magnet shapes create different stripe effects
- Base coat
- Top coat (no-wipe preferred)

### Application Steps

1. **Prep**: Clean nails, push back cuticles, lightly buff the nail surface
2. **Base coat**: Apply a thin layer of base coat, cure fully (60 sec LED)
3. **Apply cat eye gel**: Brush on a thin, even layer of cat eye color — **do not cure yet**
4. **Magnet work**: Hold the magnet wand 1–2mm above the nail for 5–10 seconds, watching the stripe form
5. **Adjust placement**: The stripe is typically centered, but angling it diagonally or off-center creates different artistic effects
6. **Cure immediately**: Once the stripe is where you want it, cure right away (60 sec LED)
7. **Repeat 2–3 layers**: Each additional layer deepens the effect
8. **Top coat**: Seal with top coat for protection and shine, cure fully

## Cat Eye Gel Care Tips

1. **Protect from sharp impacts**: While gel is durable, the magnetic particle layer is relatively delicate at extreme angles
2. **Apply protective top coat weekly**: A quick-dry nail oil over your top coat adds extra protection
3. **Professional removal only**: Cat eye gel requires acetone soak-off; do not peel or force off
4. **Wear gloves for prolonged water exposure**: Dishwashing and laundry can shorten wear time
