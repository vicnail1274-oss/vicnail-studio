## Spring 2026 Nail Colors: What Actually Sells in Salons Right Now

Every salon owner and nail technician in Taiwan is asking the same question: what colors should I stock, and what should I promote this spring?

After monitoring salon booking data, Instagram trend reports, and Pinterest search data through March 2026, here's what the numbers actually show — not just what's trending on Pinterest, but what clients are requesting and paying for.

---

## The Data: What Salon Clients Are Actually Booking

### The Top 5 Spring 2026 Requested Colors

**1. Milky White / Nude Pink (#1 requested)**

Every salon owner we spoke to for this report named milky white as their most-requested spring color. Not pure white — milky, with a slight pink or peach undertone that makes nails look clean and expensive without looking like a French manicure.

The specific shade clients want: cream-like opacity, no shimmer, slightly warm undertone.

**2. Soft Coral (#2 requested)**

Coral — not orange, not pink — is the surprise climber of spring 2026. It's replacing last year's peach as the "warm neutral" choice for clients who want something more interesting than nude but less bold than red.

**3. Pale Lavender (#3 requested)**

Purple has been trending for two seasons. Spring 2026's version is pale lavender — almost a gray-lavender — that photographs well in natural light and flatters every skin tone.

**4. Powder Blue**

Cool-toned clients who skip coral gravitate to powder blue. It's the "fresh" color of the season, replacing last year's periwinkle with something more saturated and modern.

**5. Terracotta / Earthy Orange**

Warm-toned clients are choosing terracotta over coral. The shade reads as sophisticated and pairs well with spring and summer wardrobes.

---

## What's NOT Selling

Knowing what clients aren't booking is equally important:

- **Glitter**: Glitter peaked in 2023-2024 and has been declining steadily. Clients who want sparkle are choosing shimmer polishes instead.
- **Jet Black**: Year-round available but not a spring request. Save inventory dollars elsewhere.
- **Chrome**: Chrome is now a year-round option, not a seasonal trend. No spike in spring bookings.

---

## The Color Families clients are requesting by client profile

### Young Professional (20-30)
- Milky/frosted finishes
- Nude pinks and beiges
- "Clean girl" aesthetic
- Short, rounded shapes

### Creative/Artistic (20-35)
- Terracotta, dusty blue, sage green
- Two-tone and color blocking
- Longer shapes (almond, stiletto)

### Mature Client (40+)
- Nude pinks, soft corals
- French manicure with twist (milky tips, not pure white)
- understated elegance
- Short to medium length

### Bride (seasonal, peaks April-June)
- Milky white
- Blush pink
- Tiny pearl or crystal accent on ring finger
- Barely-there shimmer

## What's Working in Marketing

The salons successfully converting spring bookings in 2026 share common traits:

1. **Lead with "milky" and "soft" in descriptions** — clients search for these words
2. **Show natural lighting photos** — outdoor, daylight, minimal editing
3. **Offer spring bundles** — 3 colors for the price of 2
4. **Book spring appointments 2-3 weeks ahead** — clients are planning for events

---

## The Bottom Line

Spring 2026 is the season of understatement. Clients want colors that look expensive and effortless, not bold or experimental. Stock milky whites, soft corals, pale lavenders, and powder blues. Skip the glitter and chrome — those are permanent categories now, not seasonal drivers.

The clients booking right now want to look polished and put-together. Serve that desire.
