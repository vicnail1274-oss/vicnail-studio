Nail art looks simple, but achieving a clean, long-lasting, professional finish takes mastering a few core fundamentals. This guide is designed for absolute beginners — covering brushwork, base coat, and the complete step-by-step process from start to finish.

---

## 1. Brushwork Basics: Why the 3-Stroke Method Matters

One of the first mistakes beginners make is loading the brush with polish and dragging it back and forth across the nail. This makes thickness control nearly impossible and leads to brush marks, air bubbles, and messy edges.

### The Correct 3-Stroke Technique

Complete each nail using exactly **three strokes**:

1. **Center stroke**: Starting near the cuticle (without touching it), pull one smooth stroke straight to the tip
2. **Left stroke**: Cover the left side, angling slightly toward the edge
3. **Right stroke**: Mirror the left side to complete even coverage

This method ensures uniform coverage and eliminates the uneven buildup caused by repeated back-and-forth strokes.

### How to Load Your Brush Correctly

- After pulling the brush from the bottle, **wipe one side against the inner rim** to remove excess polish — only one side should be loaded
- The brush tip should be slightly flat with an appropriate amount of polish, not round and bulging
- Too much polish = flooding the edges and pooling; too little = uneven coverage requiring too many passes

### Pressure Control

Press the brush lightly so the bristles spread just enough to lay flat against the nail surface. Do not push hard enough to bend the bristles. Steady, even pressure beats fast strokes every time.

## 2. Base Coat: Why You Should Never Skip It

### What Base Coat Actually Does

Many beginners think base coat is optional and skip straight to color. This is one of the most costly mistakes in nail care. Base coat serves several critical purposes:

- **Adhesion**: Gives color gel or polish something to grip, dramatically extending wear time
- **Nail protection**: Creates a barrier that prevents dark pigments from staining the natural nail yellow
- **Surface leveling**: Fills in fine ridges and texture on the nail plate for a smoother color application

### How to Apply Base Coat

1. **Prep the nail surface**: Lightly buff with a nail file, then clean with a lint-free wipe and nail dehydrator or alcohol
2. **Apply a thin, even layer**: Resist the urge to apply thickly — thin layers always outperform thick ones
3. **Leave a small gap**: Stay about 0.5mm away from the cuticle to avoid skin contact
4. **Cure under lamp**: LED lamps typically need 30–60 seconds; UV lamps need about 2 minutes
5. **No need to wipe**: Most base coats can be layered over directly after curing

## 3. Complete Step-by-Step Nail Application

Once you've got brushwork and base coat down, here is the full standard application process:

### Step 1: Nail Prep

- Shape your nails with a file (beginners: start with a soft square or oval shape)
- Push back cuticles with a cuticle pusher — avoid cutting unless necessary
- Lightly buff the nail surface to improve adhesion
- Wipe nails with a lint-free pad soaked in alcohol or nail prep solution to remove oils

### Step 2: Apply Base Coat

Apply a thin layer following the steps above and cure fully.

### Step 3: First Color Layer

- Use the 3-stroke method to apply a thin first coat of color gel
- It's normal for it to look sheer at this stage — don't try to build it in one coat
- Cure under lamp (LED: 30–60 seconds)

### Step 4: Second Color Layer

- The second coat builds full opacity and saturated color
- Apply the same 3-stroke method; focus on keeping edges clean and consistent
- Cure under lamp

### Step 5: Top Coat

- Apply a thin top coat, slightly wrapping the tip of the nail to seal the edge
- Cure under lamp
- If using a wipe-off top coat, cleanse with a lint-free wipe and cleanser to remove the inhibition layer

## 4. Common Beginner Mistakes and Fixes

| Mistake | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Bubbles in polish | Too much product on brush / shaking the bottle | Roll the bottle instead of shaking; reduce brush load |
| Polish flooding edges | Too much pressure or product | Wipe one side of brush; apply lighter pressure |
| Uneven color | Applying one thick coat | Always use 2 thin coats instead of 1 thick |
| Early peeling | Skipping base coat or oily nail surface | Clean nails thoroughly before every application |
| Cuticle skin getting coated | Painting too close to the root | Maintain a 0.5mm gap from the cuticle |

---

## 5. Practice Tips for Beginners

- **Practice on nail tips or false nails first** to build muscle memory before working on real nails
- **Focus on one skill at a time**: Week 1 is the 3-stroke method, Week 2 is clean edges
- **Take photos after each session** to track your improvement over time
- **Master solid colors before adding nail art**: A clean solid coat means any design will look better

Nail technique is a skill that builds with repetition. Get the fundamentals right, and every advanced technique becomes much easier to learn.
