Strong, healthy nails are not simply a matter of genetics — they are built through consistent care. Most people assume breakage, peeling, and brittleness are inevitable, but the truth is that 80% of nail problems can be improved with the right daily routine. Here are 7 steps to build lasting nail health.

---

## Step 1: File Correctly — Direction Is Everything

Filing seems straightforward, but doing it wrong actively damages your nails.

### The Right Technique
- **File in one direction only**: Move from each side toward the center in a single sweeping motion
- Never saw back and forth — this causes edge splitting, delamination, and weak tips

### Tool Selection
- **Beginner recommendation**: A 180/240-grit cushion file is ideal for natural nails
- Avoid metal files — they're too aggressive and thin the nail plate over time
- Glass files are the gentlest option and ideal for brittle or thin nails

### Shape Matters
Round or oval shapes are less prone to breakage than square because they eliminate the vulnerable sharp corners at the sides.

---

## Step 2: Daily Hydration — Cuticle Oil Is Non-Negotiable

Dryness is the leading cause of nail breakage. Cuticle oil is the single most effective daily hydration tool you can use.

### How to Use It
- Apply **at least once a day** — morning or before bed are ideal
- Massage into the cuticle and nail base to promote absorption
- Right after washing hands is the best window — cuticles are most depleted then

### What to Look For
- **Jojoba oil**: Small molecules absorb quickly; excellent for daily use
- **Sweet almond or olive oil**: Deeply nourishing, ideal for severely dry cuticles
- **Vitamin E**: Antioxidant properties support cuticle regeneration

DIY cuticle oil is easy: jojoba oil + a few drops of vitamin E + lavender essential oil in a roller bottle to carry with you.

---

## Step 3: Base Coat as Protection — Even on Bare Nails

Base coat isn't just the first step in a gel manicure. Even without color, applying a strengthening base coat to bare nails weekly provides significant structural support.

### Recommended Practice
- Once a week, apply a thin layer of strengthening nail treatment to clean, unpolished nails
- Water-based strengthening formulas air-dry without a UV lamp
- Use alongside cuticle oil for both surface and internal protection

---

## Step 4: Remove Gently — Aggressive Removal Does More Damage Than Wearing Too Long

The way you remove nail product has a direct impact on nail plate thickness and resilience over time.

### Correct Removal Steps
1. Lightly buff the top coat surface with a file (do not file into the nail plate itself)
2. Soak a lint-free pad in acetone or gel remover and press it against the nail
3. Wrap each finger in foil and wait 10–15 minutes
4. Gently push off the softened gel with a wooden or rubber pusher — no scraping
5. Apply cuticle oil immediately after removal

### Never Do These
- Pick or pry off gel with your other fingernails
- Force off nail tips without soaking
- Immediately start a new set without letting nails rest

## Step 5: Nail Nutrition — Your Nails Reflect Your Diet

Nails are made of keratin, and what you eat directly influences their quality, thickness, and growth rate.

### Nail-Friendly Nutrients

| Nutrient | Benefit | Food Sources |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Biotin | Increases nail plate thickness and hardness | Eggs, nuts, legumes |
| Protein | Raw material for keratin synthesis | Chicken, fish, tofu |
| Iron | Prevents nail ridging and spooning | Spinach, red meat, beans |
| Omega-3 | Keeps nails flexible and less brittle | Salmon, flaxseed, walnuts |
| Zinc | Supports nail plate growth | Pumpkin seeds, beef, oats |

## Step 6: Strengthening Treatments — For Problem Nails

If your nails are already showing signs of weakness, layering, or splitting, add these targeted strengthening practices:

### Weekly Repair Soak
- Warm water with a few drops of olive oil, soak for 10 minutes, then gently push cuticles
- Follow with a strengthening nail treatment containing hydrolyzed wheat protein or formaldehyde resin

### Silk Wraps for Cracked Nails
For severely fragile or cracked nails, a silk wrap technique bonds a thin fiber layer over the nail surface, allowing the damaged area to grow out while protected.

---

## Step 7: Break the Bad Habits

This final step is the hardest — but also the most impactful. These habits are the number one enemies of nail health:

### Habits to Eliminate
- **Nail biting**: Directly damages the nail plate and cuticle and introduces bacteria
- **Using nails as tools** (opening ring-pulls, peeling stickers, scratching): Every time thins the nail plate
- **Skipping gloves for dishes and cleaning**: Detergents and hot water are the biggest dehydration source
- **Aggressively cutting cuticles**: Cuticles are a natural seal — over-trimming increases infection risk

### Alternatives That Actually Work
- For nail biting: Apply bitter-taste nail deterrent; identify your stress triggers and replace the habit
- For housework: Keep a pair of rubber gloves hanging right at the kitchen sink

## Weekly Nail Care Schedule

| Frequency | Care Action |
|-----------|-------------|
| Daily | Cuticle oil application |
| Weekly | Filing and reshaping, strengthening base coat |
| After every removal | Cuticle oil + 1–2 days of rest |
| Monthly | Assess nail condition, adjust routine |

Nail health is cumulative. Start today — in 4 to 6 weeks, the difference will be visible.
