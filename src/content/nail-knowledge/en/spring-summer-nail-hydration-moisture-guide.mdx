When the weather heats up, most people remember sunscreen for their face — but forget that hands are just as exposed to UV rays. Add in frequent handwashing and alcohol-based sanitizers, and your nails and cuticles lose moisture much faster than you'd expect.

Spring is the perfect time to reset your nail care routine.

---

## 1. Apply Cuticle Oil Daily — Don't Wait Until You're Already Peeling

Most people only reach for cuticle oil when things have already gone wrong. That's too late.

Cuticle oil works best as **preventive care**, not emergency repair:
- Apply every night before bed and massage in until absorbed
- Re-apply after washing dishes or bathing
- Look for formulas containing **jojoba oil, vitamin E, or sweet almond oil**

The results come from consistency, not a single application. After two weeks of daily use, you'll notice a real difference in how smooth your cuticles feel.

---

## 2. After Gel Removal, Let Your Nails "Rest" for a Week

It's tempting to book your next gel set the day after removal. But doing this repeatedly makes nails progressively thinner over time.

Spring and summer are when nails grow fastest — take advantage of that by giving them a recovery period:
- Wait at least **5–7 days** before the next gel application
- During that time, apply a **nourishing clear coat or sheer nude polish**
- Continue nightly cuticle oil massage

Letting your nail plate replenish its natural oils also improves gel adhesion next time.

---

## 3. Don't Over-Remove Your Cuticles

Cuticles aren't just dead skin — they protect the base of the new nail growth.

**What to avoid:** Cutting aggressively with scissors or forcing cuticles back with rough tools.
**What to do instead:**
- Soak hands in warm water for 5 minutes to soften, then **gently push back** with a soft tool
- Only trim pieces that are genuinely lifted and at risk of tearing
- Frequency: Every two weeks is enough — you don't need to do it at every appointment

---

## 4. Sunscreen Isn't Just for Your Face — Protect Your Hands Too

UV exposure is one of the leading causes of gel color fading and premature cuticle aging.

Simple daily protection:
- Apply SPF 30+ sunscreen to the backs of your hands before driving or spending time outdoors
- After gel nails cure under a UV lamp, apply cuticle oil to counteract drying effects
- Choose a moisturizing hand cream with SPF built in — two benefits at once

---

## 5. Hydration & Diet: The Most Affordable Nail Treatment

The condition of your nails reflects your overall hydration and nutrition levels — about 80% of nail health comes from within.

Hot weather means you sweat more and dehydrate faster, which leads to brittle, breakage-prone nails. Easy fixes:
- Drink **1,500–2,000ml of water daily**
- Increase **biotin** intake: eggs, nuts, and oats are all excellent sources
- Wear gloves when doing household cleaning to avoid prolonged chemical exposure

---

## The Bottom Line

Spring and summer nail care comes down to one principle: **hydrate and protect**.

You don't need expensive products — just these five daily habits. Give it two months and your nails will speak for themselves.
