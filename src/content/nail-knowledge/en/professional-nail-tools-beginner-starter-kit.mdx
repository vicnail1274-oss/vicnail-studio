1. Nail files (multiple grits)
2. Cuticle pusher and cuticle nipper
3. UV/LED nail lamp
4. Base coat and top coat
5. Nail forms and nail tips
6. Brush set

## Tool 1: Nail Files

Nail files are the most frequently used tool in any kit. Grit numbers describe the coarseness of the abrasive surface — **lower numbers are coarser, higher numbers are finer**.

### Grit Guide

| Grit | Use |
|------|-----|
| 80–100 | Removing hard gel, shaping extensions |
| 150–180 | Shaping natural nails |
| 220–240 | Light buffing to prep nail surface |
| 300+ | Polishing and finishing |

### Recommended Starter Combination
- One 180-grit file (everyday shaping)
- One 240-grit buffer (nail prep)
- One shine buffer or polishing block (final finish)

### Buying Tips
Cushion files (foam-backed) are more flexible than stiff board files, making them gentler on natural nails. Glass nail files are long-lasting, washable, and sanitary — worth the investment if you're committed. A solid starter set costs around $8–15 USD.

## Tool 2: Cuticle Pusher and Cuticle Nipper

A cuticle pusher tidies and pushes back the cuticle area. A cuticle nipper removes excess dead skin and overgrown cuticle tissue.

### Cuticle Pusher Options
- **Wood orange sticks**: Single-use, inexpensive, great for practice
- **Stainless steel pusher**: Durable, sterilizable, the professional standard
- Technique: After softening cuticles with oil or warm water, hold the pusher at a 45-degree angle and gently push — never scrape with force

### Cuticle Nipper Selection
- Look for **precision blade quality** — low-quality nippers pinch and tear rather than cutting cleanly
- Beginners should start with a small jaw size (about 3–4mm blade) for better control
- Trusted brands: Revlon, Seki Edge, or German stainless steel options

### Important Note
Cuticles are a natural protective seal for the nail matrix. Beginners should focus on **pushing rather than cutting** — over-trimming opens the door to infection.

## Tool 3: UV/LED Nail Lamp

The nail lamp is the single most important piece of equipment in a gel nail setup — it determines whether your gel cures properly.

### UV vs LED

| Feature | UV Lamp | LED Lamp |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Cure time | 2–3 minutes | 30–60 seconds |
| Bulb lifespan | Bulbs need periodic replacement | LEDs last the life of the lamp |
| Compatibility | Works with nearly all gel formulas | Check brand compatibility |
| Price | Lower | Slightly higher |

**LED is the recommended choice for beginners** — faster curing, lower maintenance.

### What to Look for When Buying
- **Wattage**: Minimum 36W; 48W or higher is recommended
- **LED distribution**: Look for designs where all fingers cure evenly (cat-ear or tunnel designs work well)
- **Timer settings**: Switchable 30/60/99-second options
- **Low-heat mode**: Essential for thin or sensitive nails — prevents the heat spike sensation that some clients experience

### Budget Guide
- Entry-level: $25–45 USD (fully functional for learning)
- Mid-range: $60–120 USD (faster, more even curing, better build quality)

## Tool 4: Base Coat and Top Coat

Base coat and top coat are the bookends of every gel manicure — don't cut corners on either.

- **Base coat**: Choose from the same brand as your color gel whenever possible for maximum adhesion compatibility
- **Top coat types**:
  - **Wipe-off top coat**: Requires cleansing the inhibition layer after curing with a lint-free wipe and cleanser; produces a glass-like, long-lasting shine
  - **No-wipe top coat**: Cure and done — more convenient and beginner-friendly

A base-and-top-coat bundle from the same brand eliminates compatibility guesswork and is the smartest way to start.

## Tool 5: Nail Forms and Nail Tips

### Nail Tips
- Thin plastic pieces in clear or natural tones used to extend the nail length
- **Full-cover tips** cover the entire nail; **half-well tips** bond only to the free edge
- Full-cover tips are ideal for beginners practicing nail art designs without waiting for natural nail growth

### Nail Forms
- Paper or foil guides that stick under the free edge as a scaffolding surface for sculpting extensions
- More technique-intensive than tips — recommended once basic application skills are established

## Tool 6: Brush Set

Good brushes make the difference between clean, intentional nail art and muddy, frustrating results.

| Brush Type | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Flat brush | Applying base coat, color gel, and gel layers |
| Liner / detail brush | Thin lines, patterns, nail art details |
| Dotting tool | Dots, petals, water-drop designs |
| Fan brush | Gradient effects, dusting loose pigment |

Beginner brush sets typically include all the basics listed above. A quality 5–8 piece set runs $10–25 USD and will cover most beginner designs.

---

## Tool 7: Nail Cleanser and Dehydrator

### Nail Dehydrator
- Applied to the nail plate before base coat
- Removes surface oils and moisture that reduce adhesion
- One of the simplest ways to prevent lifting and extend wear time

### Nail Cleanser / Gel Cleanser
- Removes the tacky inhibition layer after curing certain top coats and base coats
- Also effective for cleaning brushes

---

## Tool 8: Lint-Free Wipes

Regular cotton balls and makeup pads shed fibers that stick to uncured gel, leaving a rough, textured surface. **Lint-free wipes are non-negotiable in a nail kit** — do not substitute with cotton balls.

Look for pads marketed as "nail wipes," "lint-free nail pads," or "prep wipes." A pack of 500 typically costs around $5–8 USD and lasts a long time.
