## The 10 Most Common Nail Mistakes

Even experienced nail enthusiasts can fall into these traps without realizing it. Identify the issue, apply the fix, and watch your work improve dramatically.

## Mistake 1: Applying Base Coat Too Thick

**Symptom**: Uneven surface after curing — bubbles or ripples.

**Fix**: Base coat should go on thin and even. Ensure full coverage in every corner, but never let it pool or build up.

## Mistake 2: Gradient Sponge Not Properly Saturated

**Symptom**: Murky, undefined gradient with blended colors turning muddy.

**Fix**: Test the sponge on paper a few times before touching the nail. Work in thin, light layers and build gradually.

## Mistake 3: Gel Touching the Cuticle Skin

**Symptom**: Nails lift unusually fast, starting from the cuticle edge.

**Fix**: Leave a 0.5–1mm gap between the gel and the surrounding skin. Gel must never touch skin — doing so dramatically reduces adhesion after curing.

## Mistake 4: Insufficient Curing Time

**Symptom**: Sticky surface after curing; nail impressions and dents appear easily.

**Fix**: Match your lamp wattage to the brand's recommended cure time. LED lamps typically need 30–60 seconds; UV lamps need around 120 seconds. Always check the instructions.

## Mistake 5: Peeling Off Gel Nails

**Symptom**: Natural nail surface is stripped away, leaving thin, brittle nails.

**Fix**: Always soak off using the foil-wrap method (cotton pad soaked in acetone + foil) for 10–15 minutes. Gently push off softened gel — never force or peel.

## Mistake 6: Poor Proportion in Nail Art

**Symptom**: Design elements look too large or off-center, creating an awkward visual balance.

**Fix**: Sketch the outline with a fine brush before filling in. Design elements should cover no more than 60% of the nail surface — white space lets your art breathe.

## Mistake 7: Incomplete Edge Sealing with Top Coat

**Symptom**: The nail tip shows color wear and chipping first.

**Fix**: On the final stroke of top coat, "cap" the free edge by running the brush along the tip of the nail to seal it completely.

## Mistake 8: Over-Filing the Natural Nail

**Symptom**: Natural nail becomes thin, sensitive, and nearly transparent.

**Fix**: A 120–180 grit file is all you need for surface prep — just enough to remove shine. Never apply pressure or buff the same spot repeatedly.

## Mistake 9: Mixing Incompatible Brands

**Symptom**: Poor adhesion and early lifting across the entire set.

**Fix**: Use base coat, color gel, and top coat from the same product line where possible. If mixing brands is unavoidable, test compatibility on a single nail first.

## Mistake 10: Skipping Prep Steps

**Symptom**: Nails begin falling off within one week of application.

**Fix**: Prep is everything — cuticle removal, dehydration, light buffing, and a quality base coat are all non-negotiable steps. Never skip any of them.

Every one of these mistakes is fixable. Diagnose the pattern, practice deliberately, and your nail work will keep getting better.
