## Extensions Need More Daily Care Than Regular Gel Nails

Because gel extensions go beyond the natural nail tip, they're exposed to significantly more mechanical stress than a standard gel manicure. Without the right daily habits, you can expect lifting, cracking, or breakage within two weeks.

Here are 5 habits that make the biggest difference.

---

## Habit 1: Always Wear Waterproof Gloves for Chores

Dishwashing, bathroom cleaning, and handling detergents are the top enemies of nail extensions. Prolonged water exposure weakens the bond between the gel and your natural nail, leading to lifting.

**How to do it**: Keep a pair of latex or silicone gloves near the sink. Put them on for any task involving water or cleaning products — even short ones.

---

## Habit 2: Stop Using Your Nails as Tools

Prying open lids, popping staples, peeling stickers — these actions are low-risk for natural nails but high-risk for extensions. Direct impact can cause breakage; sideways force often causes lifting at the edges.

**How to do it**: Use your fingertips or a tool (like a bottle opener) instead of your nail tips for applying force. Train yourself out of the "use fingernails for everything" habit.

---

## Habit 3: Moisturize Your Cuticles and Nail Edges Daily

Gel extensions can accelerate moisture loss from your natural nails. Dry, cracking cuticles aren't just a cosmetic issue — they allow microscopic gaps to form along the nail edge, increasing the risk of water infiltration and lifting.

**How to do it**: Apply cuticle oil or hand cream to the nail edges and surrounding skin twice a day — morning and night. Olive oil or jojoba oil work well as budget-friendly alternatives.

---

## Habit 4: Dry Your Nails Immediately After Soaking

After long soaks in hot water — baths, hot springs, foot soaks — your natural nail expands from heat while the gel extension doesn't. This creates tension at the bond line and accelerates lifting.

**How to do it**: Gently but thoroughly dry the spaces around and under your extensions with a towel right after soaking. Try to avoid soaking for longer than 15 minutes at a time.

---

## Habit 5: Get a Fill Every 3–4 Weeks Without Fail

As your natural nail grows, the extension's center of gravity shifts outward, increasing the leverage force on the bond. Going beyond four weeks without a fill dramatically raises your risk of breakage and lifting.

**How to do it**: Book a fill appointment every 3–4 weeks, even if your extensions look intact. Your technician will rebalance the extension and reinforce any weak spots during the fill.

---

## Quick Reference Summary

| Habit | What It Protects Against |
|-------|--------------------------|
| Wear gloves for chores | Water and chemical damage |
| No nails-as-tools | Breaking and edge lifting |
| Daily cuticle moisturizing | Gap formation from dryness |
| Dry nails after soaking | Thermal expansion lifting |
| 3–4 week fill schedule | Structural imbalance and breakage |

Build these 5 habits into your routine and you'll reliably get 4-week wear out of your extensions — while keeping your natural nails healthy underneath.
