## Setting Up Your Nail Workspace

Having the right tools from the start makes learning nail art far less frustrating. This list gives you a solid, beginner-friendly toolkit without unnecessary extras.

## The Essential 10 (Must-Have)

### 1. LED/UV Curing Lamp
The core of any gel nail setup. Choose at least 36W with a built-in timer. LED lamps cure faster; UV lamps have broader compatibility with different gel brands.

### 2. Base Coat
The first layer that protects the natural nail and anchors the gel. Look for an anti-lift formula for best results.

### 3. Color Gel
Start with 5–10 shades. A beginner palette should include nude, pink, white, and black as foundation colors.

### 4. Top Coat
The final protective layer that adds shine and seals your design. Have both a glossy and a matte top coat on hand.

### 5. Nail Brush Set
At minimum: a flat brush (for color application), a fine liner brush (for nail art), and a round-head brush (for spreading gel).

### 6. Nail Files (Multiple Grits)
120–180 grit for surface prep, 240 grit for shaping, 300+ grit for buffing. At least three grits to start.

### 7. Cuticle Pusher and Dead Skin Remover
Softens and pushes back the cuticle to create a cleaner nail surface for application.

### 8. Alcohol Wipes or Nail Cleanser
Essential for pre-application degreasing and for wiping the sticky inhibition layer off gel after curing.

### 9. Foil Wraps + Remover Cotton Pads
Standard supplies for the soak-off removal method — required for any gel removal session.

### 10. Practice Nail Tips
Strongly recommended for beginners. Practice on fake tips so you're not learning on your own nails every time.

## The Advanced 5 (Highly Recommended)

### 11. Gel Thinner (Diluent)
Reduces gel viscosity to make gradients and detailed nail art significantly easier to blend.

### 12. Dotting Tool
Creates perfectly sized dots for florals and accents — much easier to control than a brush for this purpose.

### 13. Nail Stickers and Foil Transfer
A quick way to add visual interest without hand-painting skills. Great for beginners who want complex-looking results.

### 14. Electric Nail Drill (E-file)
Speeds up buffing and cuticle cleanup considerably — but requires practice to master the right pressure.

### 15. Cuticle Oil
Post-manicure daily care essential. Keeps the cuticle hydrated and extends the life of your manicure.

## Shopping Tips

- **Lamp**: Don't go below 24W — insufficient curing is one of the top causes of early lifting
- **Gel**: Stick to reputable brands initially; cheap no-name gels create inconsistent results
- **Brushes**: Medium-firmness nylon bristles give the best control for beginners

With these 15 tools, you'll have a complete home nail station. Start on practice tips, build your muscle memory, and you'll be creating sets you're proud of sooner than you think.
