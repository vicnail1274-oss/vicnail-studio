Want longer nails? TIPS overlays and gel extensions are the two most popular ways to get there. Both deliver length fast, but they differ in technique, appearance, maintenance, and cost. Here's a complete breakdown to help you choose before your next appointment.

---

## What Are TIPS Nails?

TIPS use pre-made curved plastic tips that a nail technician attaches to the front of your natural nail using adhesive or gel. A layer of gel is then applied on top to shape and seal the extension.

**Key features of TIPS:**
- Faster application: typically 1.5–2 hours for a full set
- Works well for short nail beds, irregular shapes, or bitten nails
- Wide range of length and shape options (from short oval to extra-long square)
- Requires a fill or new set every 3–4 weeks

---

## What Are Gel Extensions?

Gel extensions build length directly on your natural nail using a nail form or extension clip and builder gel — no pre-made tip needed. The technician sculpts the shape entirely by hand.

**Key features of gel extensions:**
- Longer application time: approximately 2–3 hours
- More natural appearance with a thinner, more seamless profile
- Higher flexibility — less likely to snap off as a single piece
- Fill every 3–4 weeks; removal requires more filing time

---

## Which Should You Choose?

**Go with TIPS if you:**
- Are trying nail extensions for the first time
- Have a tight schedule and need a faster appointment
- Are working within a tighter budget
- Have short nail beds and need the extra surface area a tip provides

**Go with gel extensions if you:**
- Value a natural look that mimics real nail growth
- Use your hands frequently and need maximum durability
- Plan to maintain gel nails long-term and care about overall quality
- Prefer the feel of no seam between tip and natural nail
