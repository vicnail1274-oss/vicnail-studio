## Why Do Gel Nails Chip More in Spring?

Spring is a beautiful season—but it's also one of the most challenging times for gel nails. As temperatures rise and humidity fluctuates, the nail plate itself expands and contracts slightly, weakening the bond between the gel layer and the nail surface. Add in increased outdoor activities and more frequent hand washing, and it's no surprise that chipping and lifting become more common.

Understanding how spring affects your gel nails is the first step to better care. Here are five key strategies to prevent chipping and keep your manicure looking perfect through the season.

---

## 1. Nail Prep Matters More in Spring

Spring brings increased sebum (natural oil) production on the skin, including the nail surface. If this oil isn't fully removed before gel application, the gel will begin lifting from the edges shortly after curing. Before your gel appointment, make sure your nail technician thoroughly cleanses each nail with a dehydrator or alcohol wipe to ensure a clean, oil-free surface.

It also helps to lightly buff the nail surface to increase adhesion area. Just be careful not to over-file—thin nails are weaker and more prone to breakage.

**Pro tip:** After buffing, avoid touching the nail surface with bare hands. Even clean fingers carry oils that can interfere with gel bonding.

---

## 2. Edge Sealing Is the Core of Chipping Prevention

"Capping the free edge" is one of the most overlooked yet critical steps in gel application. It means running the brush along the tip of each nail at every layer—base coat, color coats, and top coat—so the gel wraps around and encases the nail edge rather than just sitting on the surface.

In spring, consider asking your nail technician to apply an extra layer of top coat specifically on the nail tips. This creates a stronger seal that prevents moisture from seeping between the gel and the nail plate—the main cause of lifting and chipping.

---

## 3. Hydration Is Non-Negotiable

Spring air can still be dry, and frequent hand washing or sanitizer use strips moisture from the skin around your nails. Dry, cracked cuticles don't just look unsightly—they're a common starting point for gel lifting and even small skin tears.

**Daily hydration routine:**
- Apply cuticle oil morning and night, massaging gently around the nail bed and cuticle area
- Look for products with jojoba oil, sweet almond oil, or vitamin E for fast absorption without greasiness
- Wear gloves when doing dishes or cleaning—hot water and detergents are hard on gel
- Dry hands thoroughly after washing, paying attention to the spaces around the nail edges

Cuticle oil actually penetrates through the gel layer to hydrate from beneath, keeping the gel flexible and less prone to cracking.

---

## 4. Avoid These Spring High-Risk Behaviors

Warmer weather brings more activities—and more risks to your gel nails:

- **Gardening and outdoor activities:** Soil and grit damage gel edges. Always wear gloves
- **Prolonged soaking:** Swimming or long baths cause the gel layer to absorb water and swell, reducing longevity
- **Using nails as tools:** Peeling tape, popping cans, or picking at things with your nails leads to breakage fast
- **Ignoring sun protection:** While UV lamps cure gel properly, extended exposure to intense outdoor sunlight can cause some gel colors to fade slightly over time

Being aware of these risks and taking simple precautions can significantly extend the life of your manicure.

---

## 5. When to Infill and When to Remove

Even with perfect care, the ideal maintenance cycle for gel nails remains **2–3 weeks**. If you notice significant lifting or a chip, visit your nail technician promptly for an infill or fresh set. Never pick or force-peel lifting gel—this strips layers from the natural nail and leads to thin, damaged nails.

When it's time to remove gel completely, always use a proper gel remover (acetone soak) and have a professional gently push off the softened gel. Rushing removal is the fastest way to damage your nails.

## Spring Gel Nail Care Summary

| Care Step | Recommended Frequency |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Cuticle oil massage | Morning & evening daily |
| Wear gloves for housework | Every time you use cleaning products |
| Infill or new set | Every 2–3 weeks |

Lasting gel nails in spring come down to three things working together: **proper prep**, **daily hydration**, and **avoiding high-risk habits**. Master these three pillars and your nails will look great no matter what spring throws at them.
