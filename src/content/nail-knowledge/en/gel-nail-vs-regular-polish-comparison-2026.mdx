## Introduction: Which Is Better — Gel or Regular Polish?

"I want to get my nails done, but should I go with gel or regular polish?"

This is one of the most common questions from nail beginners. Both enhance your nails, but they differ significantly in durability, application process, cost, and nail health impact. This guide compares them across 6 key areas to help you make the best choice for your needs.

---

## What Are Gel Nails? What Is Regular Nail Polish?

**Gel nails** use a special formula containing photoinitiators that cure and harden under UV or LED light. The result is a high-gloss, chip-resistant finish that lasts far longer than regular polish.

**Regular nail polish** (traditional lacquer) is a pigment suspended in solvent that air-dries after application. No tools required, removal is simple, but durability is comparatively shorter.

---

## 6-Point Comparison

### Category 1: Durability

| | Gel Nails | Regular Polish |
|--|---------|----------|
| Average lifespan | 2–4 weeks | 3–7 days |
| Chip resistance | Excellent | Low (easy to chip) |
| Water resistance | Excellent | Moderate |

Gel's chemical bonding structure makes it significantly more durable. For occasions requiring lasting, polished nails (weddings, travel, important meetings), gel wins clearly.

### Category 2: Visual Effect

Gel finishes are glossier and more vibrant, maintaining that high-shine look throughout the wear period.

Regular polish looks great right after application, but over time tends to:
- Lose shine and turn matte
- Chip at the free edge
- Show uneven color

### Category 3: Application Difficulty

| | Gel Nails | Regular Polish |
|--|---------|----------|
| Equipment needed | UV/LED lamp | None |
| DIY-friendly | Requires practice | Easy and accessible |
| Learning curve | Higher | Low |

Regular polish is accessible to anyone — no equipment, no preparation. DIY gel nails require purchasing a lamp and learning proper technique to get good results.

### Category 4: Cost

| | Gel Nails | Regular Polish |
|--|---------|----------|
| Salon cost (Taiwan) | NT$500–1,500/session | NT$200–400/session |
| DIY starter cost | NT$2,000–5,000 (lamp + gel kit) | NT$200–500 (a few polishes) |
| Removal cost | Requires materials (salon or DIY) | Free (acetone remover) |

Short-term, regular polish is cheaper. But when you consider **cost per wear day**, gel often offers better value since it lasts 3–4 times longer.

### Category 5: Removal Process

**Regular nail polish**: Acetone-based remover dissolves it in minutes with minimal nail impact (when used appropriately).

**Gel nails**: Requires buffing the top coat, soaking in pure acetone for 10–15 minutes to soften, then gently pushing off with a wooden stick. Total process: 20–30 minutes. **Forcing or peeling gel off causes serious nail damage.**

> ⚠️ Important: Regardless of gel type, always remove gel properly. Never peel or force it off.

### Category 6: Nail Health Impact

With correct application and removal, neither option causes significant nail damage. Key details to watch:

| Damage Cause | Gel | Regular Polish |
|---------|------|----------|
| Over-buffing | Possible (prep step) | Less common |
| Forced removal | Severe nail damage | Moderate damage |
| Long-term coverage concerns | Debated | Less concern |
| Allergic reactions | Some people (HEMA sensitivity) | Rare (mainly acetone irritation) |

The idea that "nails need to breathe" is a myth — nail plate is dead keratin with no gas exchange. Real damage comes from **improper technique**, especially forced removal.

## How to Choose Based on Your Needs

### Choose Gel Nails if You…

- Want nails that stay beautiful for 3+ weeks without touch-ups
- Need polished nails for events or important occasions
- Don't mind paying more for longer-lasting results
- Use your hands a lot and need chip-resistant nails
- Want a more refined, high-gloss finish

### Choose Regular Polish if You…

- Just want occasional color changes
- Are on a tight budget or prefer DIY
- Like switching colors frequently (weekly)
- Have thin, fragile nails and are concerned about buffing
- Have sensitivities to gel materials

---

## Is There a Middle Ground?

Yes! Here are two options that fall between the two:

**1. Gel Polish**
Looks similar to regular polish but with a gel-like formula that requires lamp curing. Lasts about 1–2 weeks — better than regular polish, shorter than full gel. Easier to remove than hard gel systems.

**2. Semi-Permanent Polish**
No lamp needed, lasts about 1–2 weeks. A great option if you want longer-lasting color without investing in lamp equipment.

## FAQ

**Q: Do gel nails damage nails more than regular polish?**

A: With proper application and removal, gel is no more damaging than regular polish. The main causes of nail damage are over-buffing, forced removal, and not giving nails recovery time between services.

**Q: Can I get my nails wet right after gel application?**

A: Cured gel is water-resistant, but avoid prolonged soaking for the first 24 hours to allow the gel to fully stabilize.

**Q: Can I apply regular nail polish on top of gel nails?**

A: Yes — use an acetone-free polish. Acetone will dissolve the gel underneath.

**Q: Does gel always require buffing the nail?**

A: Light buffing (just removing the shine) is standard. Heavy drilling isn't necessary for most gel systems. Frequent aggressive buffing is what thins nails over time.

## Summary

Neither gel nor regular polish is universally "better" — it's about what suits your lifestyle best:

- **Want durability and a refined finish** → Go with gel nails
- **Want flexibility and low cost** → Stick with regular polish
- **Want something in between** → Try gel polish or semi-permanent polish

Hope this comparison helps you find the right choice for your nails!
