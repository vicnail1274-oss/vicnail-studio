Most people pick nail colors by gut feeling — and then leave the salon feeling like something's slightly off. It's rarely the color itself that's the problem. It's usually **how the colors interact with each other and with your skin tone.**

Here's a practical introduction to nail color theory so every color decision has logic behind it.

---

## Start With the Color Wheel: Three Core Combinations

The color wheel is the foundation of all color pairing. Nail techs use three main approaches:

### 1. Monochromatic (Same Color Family)
One hue, varied in depth and saturation to create layers of interest.

**Example:** Soft pink × rose red × nude blush
- Great for a gentle, long-wearing aesthetic
- The lowest-risk approach — hardest to get wrong
- 2026 spring/summer pick: milk tea tones (cocoa brown × caramel × cream white)

### 2. Complementary (Opposite Colors)
Colors directly across from each other on the color wheel. High contrast, strong visual impact.

**Example:** Orange × blue / Purple × yellow
- Ideal for bold, personality-forward designs
- Rule of thumb: dominant color at 70%, accent at 30%
- Watch out: wrong proportions can look cluttered

### 3. Analogous (Neighboring Colors)
Two to three colors sitting next to each other on the color wheel. Naturally harmonious.

**Example:** Mint green × sky blue × periwinkle / Coral × rose pink
- A favorite in Japanese and Korean nail aesthetics
- Fresh, effortless, very hard to fail
- 2026 spring/summer pick: mint green × soft peach series

---

## Your Skin Tone Changes Everything

Color theory is step one. But there's a critical variable that's often overlooked: **your skin tone.**

### Warm/Yellow Undertones (Common in East Asian skin)
- Flattering: earth tones (caramel, cocoa), rose nude, deep berry red, navy blue
- Can wash out or look ashy: pure yellow, grass green, olive

### Fair/Neutral Undertones
- Almost everything works — especially pink, lavender, mint
- Avoid: all-white alone, which can look washed out

### Tan/Olive/Deep Undertones
- Flattering: bold saturated colors (royal blue, coral, true red), gold tones
- Surprisingly beautiful: deep plum, dark burgundy, copper gold

## Three Rules to Remember

Once you understand the basics, these three principles are all you need:

1. **Monochromatic = safe. Complementary = bold. Analogous = harmonious.** — choose based on the vibe you want.
2. **70% dominant color + 30% accent** — proportion is everything.
3. **Skin tone first, design second** — the most beautiful color is the one that works on *you*.
