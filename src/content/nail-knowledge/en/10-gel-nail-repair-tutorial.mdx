Even the most durable gel nails can chip or lift. Knowing what to do — and what to avoid — makes the difference between a quick fix and damaged natural nails. Here's how to handle the three most common situations.

---

## Situation 1: Minor Chip (Small Break)

If the chip is small and the gel isn't lifting, you can do a temporary home fix to buy time:

1. **Smooth the sharp edge** — Use a 180-grit file to gently even out the jagged area. This prevents it from snagging on fabric and getting larger.
2. **Apply a clear top coat** — Brush on a thin layer of UV top coat over the chip and cure it. This seals the area temporarily.
3. **Book a repair within the week** — Home fixes are only temporary. A proper repair from your technician is still needed.

⚠️ **Avoid** applying regular nail glue to gel nails. Standard glue is incompatible with gel and can cause the entire layer to detach sooner.

---

## Situation 2: Lifting Edge

Lifting means the gel is separating from the nail. The most important rule: **never peel or force the gel off** — doing so strips the surface layer of your natural nail.

**What to do:**
1. Use a clean wooden stick to gently press a mildly lifted edge back down (works only for very minor lifting)
2. Apply a small drop of cuticle oil along the gap to slow moisture from getting underneath
3. Book an appointment promptly — your technician can re-seal the edge or do a localized repair before it gets worse

---

## Situation 3: Full Gel Removal

When you're ready to change your look or the gel has run its course, follow these steps for a safe soak-off removal.

### What You'll Need
- 180-grit nail file
- Cotton pads
- Aluminum foil (cut into 10cm × 10cm squares)
- Acetone-based nail remover
- Wooden cuticle stick

### Step-by-Step

**Step 1: Break the seal**
Gently file each nail's surface until the shine disappears. You're only removing the top coat layer — do not file down to the natural nail.

**Step 2: Wrap and soak**
Saturate a cotton pad with remover, place it on the nail, and wrap tightly with foil. Leave in place for 15–20 minutes.

**Step 3: Slide off the gel**
Remove the wrap and use a wooden stick to gently push off the softened gel. If any gel remains, re-wrap for another 5 minutes. Never scrape with force.

**Step 4: Nourish immediately**
Acetone dehydrates the nail. Apply cuticle oil and a nail strengthener right away to restore moisture.

---

## Common Removal Mistakes

| Mistake | Result |
|---------|--------|
| Peeling gel off by force | Nail surface damage, pitting |
| Not soaking long enough | Gel stays hard, same damage from scraping |
| Skipping the filing step | Remover can't penetrate the top coat |
| No aftercare | Dry, brittle nails prone to breaking |

---

## Bottom Line

Correct repair and removal habits protect your natural nails for the long term. When something goes wrong, a quick home fix buys you time — then get to your nail technician for a proper repair. Your nails will stay healthier for it.
