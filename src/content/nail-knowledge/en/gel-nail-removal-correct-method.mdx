## Why Proper Gel Nail Removal Matters

Gel is designed to stay on — so forcing it off strips the top layer of your nail plate, leaving nails thin and brittle. Removing gel correctly protects nail thickness and keeps the nail bed healthy.

## Home Gel Nail Removal: Complete Step-by-Step

### What You Need

- Pure acetone
- Aluminum foil (cut into squares)
- Cotton balls or pads
- Nail file (180 grit or higher)
- Orangewood stick or cuticle pusher
- Vaseline or thick hand cream

### Step 1: Protect the Skin Around Your Nails

Apply a thin layer of Vaseline around each nail to prevent the acetone from over-drying your skin. This step is especially important if your hands are already dry.

### Step 2: File the Top Coat

Use a 180-grit file to lightly buff the gel surface until the shine disappears and the surface looks matte. This breaks through the top coat's moisture barrier so acetone can penetrate.

**Important**: You only need to break the seal — don't file all the way through the gel. Over-filing thins the nail plate.

### Step 3: Soak in Acetone

1. Soak a cotton ball in pure acetone and place it directly on the nail
2. Wrap tightly with aluminum foil so the cotton stays in firm contact
3. Wait **15–20 minutes**

Higher acetone concentration and warmth speed up the process. You can rest your wrapped hands under a warm towel.

### Step 4: Gently Push Off the Gel

Unwrap one finger at a time. Use an orangewood stick to gently push the softened gel from the cuticle toward the tip. Well-soaked gel will slide off like a soft rubber sheet.

If the gel doesn't budge, rewrap and wait another 5–10 minutes. Never force it.

### Step 5: Remove Any Remaining Gel

For small patches of residue, use a 220-grit file to lightly buff in one direction. Keep movements light and consistent — avoid back-and-forth scrubbing.

### Step 6: Nourish Your Nails

Nails are more vulnerable right after removal. Do this immediately:
- Gently push back cuticles with a pusher
- Apply nail strengthener or cuticle oil
- Massage a rich hand cream until fully absorbed

## Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

**Peeling off the gel**: The most damaging habit. Even if an edge is lifting, peeling takes nail plate layers with it.

**Pushing too soon**: If the acetone hasn't fully softened the gel, you're essentially peeling. Wait longer instead of forcing it.

**Over-filing**: File only to break the top coat, not to remove all the gel. Stop when the shine is gone.

**Doing new nails immediately**: Give your nail plate 1–2 days to recover. If you need to re-apply right away, use a nail strengthener as a base layer first.

---

## Post-Removal Care

Nails are typically dry and prone to breakage after gel removal. Maintain this routine for 2+ weeks:

- Apply cuticle oil or nail strengthener every night before bed
- Wear rubber gloves for cleaning and dishwashing
- Consider biotin supplements to support nail growth
- Avoid using nails as tools (opening cans, peeling tape, etc.)

---

## Summary

Proper gel removal comes down to three principles: **soften fully before pushing, never peel, and care for nails afterward**. Follow these steps and your nails will bounce back quickly after every removal.
