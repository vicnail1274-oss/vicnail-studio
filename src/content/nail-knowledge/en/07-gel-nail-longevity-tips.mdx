## Introduction

After getting gel nails, the most frustrating question is: "Why are my nails already lifting and fading after just one week?" The truth is, whether your gel nails last three weeks or more depends not only on your nail technician's skill but also on your daily care habits. Here are five key tips to help you get the most out of every gel nail appointment.

---

## 1. Preparation Is Everything

Many people assume gel nail results depend entirely on the application process, but the prep work beforehand is actually the foundation of longevity.

- **Remove excess cuticles**: If the cuticle area isn't properly cleaned, gel will easily peel from the edges. Use a cuticle softener 1–2 days before your appointment and gently push back the skin.
- **Degrease the nail plate**: Natural oils are the biggest enemy of gel adhesion. Before application, thoroughly clean the nail surface with an alcohol wipe or specialized degreaser to ensure a tight bond.
- **Don't over-file**: Light filing improves adhesion, but filing too thin makes nails brittle and prone to breakage.

---

## 2. Edge Sealing Is Non-Negotiable

Lifting is the most common gel nail issue, and the root cause is usually unsealed edges. Every layer—base coat, color coat, and top coat—must cap the free edge to create a complete seal. This prevents water and cleaning agents from seeping in and causing separation.

---

## 3. Daily Care: Oil Protects, Water Is the Enemy

Gel nails are most vulnerable to prolonged water exposure—dishwashing, bathing, swimming. Water causes the nail plate to expand, creating gaps between the gel and the natural nail. Here's what to do:

- **Wear gloves when washing dishes**: This single habit is the most effective way to maintain gel longevity.
- **Apply cuticle oil daily**: Cuticle oil doesn't just protect the skin—the oil molecules seep into tiny edge gaps, reducing moisture intrusion while keeping nails looking glossy.
- **Avoid using nails as tools**: Prying open lids or caps is a common cause of gel chipping at the tips.

---

## 4. Choose the Right Top Coat for Maximum Results

Top coats come in "no-wipe" and "wipe-off" varieties, plus specialty formulas like "ultra-hard" or "rubber base" top coats. If your work involves frequent contact with water or chemicals, opt for an ultra-hard top coat—it offers stronger adhesion and longer-lasting shine.

---

## 5. Proper Removal Means Better Results Next Time

Many people find their nails getting thinner and more prone to lifting over time. The culprit is usually forceful removal. Always follow the correct gel removal procedure:

1. Lightly file the top coat surface with a 180-grit buffer to break the shine layer
2. Wrap each finger with aluminum foil over an acetone-soaked cotton pad and let it sit for 15–20 minutes
3. Gently push off the softened gel with a wooden stick—never peel or force it off

After proper removal, the nail surface stays smooth and undamaged, providing better adhesion for the next gel application.
