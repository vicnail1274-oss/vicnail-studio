Did your gel manicure start lifting or peeling just days after the appointment? You're not alone — but peeling gel nails are never "normal." In most cases, there's a clear reason behind it that can be fixed. Here are the **6 most common causes of gel nail peeling** and exactly what to do about each one.

---

## Cause 1: Inadequate Nail Prep

Up to **70% of gel nail longevity comes down to prep**. If there's any oil, moisture, or cuticle residue on the nail surface, the gel simply won't bond properly.

**How to fix it:**
- Thoroughly cleanse the nail plate with an alcohol wipe before applying gel.
- Lightly buff the surface with a nail file to remove shine and improve adhesion (no need to over-file).
- Push back and remove cuticles completely — any leftover skin will cause lifting.

---

## Cause 2: Skipping or Under-Applying Base Coat

The base coat is the "bridge" between your natural nail and the gel color. Rushing through this step — or skipping it — leaves no proper foundation for the gel to grip.

**How to fix it:**
- Apply base coat to cover the entire nail plate with no gaps.
- Cure fully under the lamp (30–60 seconds for LED; longer for UV).
- Never touch oil or water before the base coat is cured.

---

## Cause 3: Not Capping the Free Edge

If the gel isn't "capped" or sealed along the tip of the nail (the free edge), water and friction will sneak in from the edge — causing peeling from the tip inward.

**How to fix it:**
- Seal the free edge with every single layer: base coat, color gel, and top coat.
- This single step is one of the easiest ways to add days (or weeks) to your manicure.

---

## Cause 4: Insufficient Curing Time

Gel that isn't fully cured may look dry on the surface but remain soft underneath. This leads to early peeling and poor durability.

**How to fix it:**
- Match your curing times to your specific gel brand (not all gels are the same).
- Check that your lamp is working properly — UV bulbs degrade over time and should be replaced approximately every 6 months.
- Keep your hand completely still during curing for even light exposure.

---

## Cause 5: Applying Layers That Are Too Thick

Thick gel layers prevent UV/LED light from penetrating all the way through, creating a "false cure" — hard on top, soft underneath — which causes the whole layer to separate.

**How to fix it:**
- Apply color gel in **two thin coats**, curing between each layer.
- Resist the temptation to apply a thick coat for more coverage; thin layers always last longer.

---

## Cause 6: Damaging Daily Habits

Even a perfect gel application can fail if your daily habits work against it. Using your nails as tools, frequent contact with harsh chemicals, or prolonged hot water soaks all break down the gel bond.

**How to fix it:**
- Wear gloves when doing dishes or cleaning with chemicals.
- Use your fingertips, not your nails, for everyday tasks.
- Avoid soaking your hands in hot water for extended periods within the first 24 hours after application.

---

## The Gel Nail Longevity Formula

> **Thorough Prep + Cap Every Edge + Thin Layers + Full Cure + Protect Daily**

Follow these five principles consistently and your gel manicure can easily last three to four weeks. If peeling persists after addressing all of the above, consider evaluating the quality of your gel products or consulting a professional nail technician for a personalized assessment.
