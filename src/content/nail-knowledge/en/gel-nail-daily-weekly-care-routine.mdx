## Getting Gel Nails Is the Start, Not the Finish

Most people treat the salon appointment as the finish line. But the filing and soaking involved in gel application actually dehydrates your nail plate. Without maintenance after the fact, you'll see dry cuticles and increased lifting risk within a week.

The good news: a sustainable routine is genuinely simple. Two habits are all it takes.

## Every Day: One Step — Apply Cuticle Oil

This single action delivers the highest return of any gel nail maintenance practice.

**Why it matters:**

Your nail plate grows from the nail matrix, which is surrounded by the cuticle area at the base and sides of your nail. When this area stays hydrated, the new nail that grows in is healthier, more flexible, and bonds better with gel. Dry, cracked cuticles are the number one predictor of premature lifting — the gel starts separating at the edges first.

**How to do it:**

1. Once daily minimum — right after washing hands while skin is still slightly damp
2. Apply a small drop to each cuticle (nail base, both sides, and the skin-to-nail junction)
3. Massage gently for 10–15 seconds to help absorption
4. No rinsing needed — let it absorb naturally

**What to look for in a cuticle oil:**

- **Jojoba oil**: Molecularly closest to natural skin sebum, absorbs quickly without greasiness — the industry standard
- **Apricot kernel oil**: Lighter feel, good for people who don't like the oily texture
- **Vitamin E**: Great for repair, but thick — best blended with other oils
- Most commercial cuticle oils combine these; a reputable brand formulation works well

Best timing: before bed. Skin repairs itself at 3× the rate during sleep.

## Every Week: Three Steps for Deep Maintenance

Once a week, block out 10 minutes for these three actions. They'll significantly extend how polished and intact your gel looks over its lifespan.

### Step 1: Cuticle Exfoliation

Overgrown cuticle buildup along the nail edge can physically push against the gel and cause lifting — not because the gel failed, but because skin is crowding it out.

How to do it:
- After a shower or hand soak (when skin is soft), use a silicone cuticle pusher to gently push back loose skin
- Only move what's already detached — don't force it
- Avoid metal pushers and scissors; microscopic cuts increase infection risk

### Step 2: Edge Inspection

Spend one minute checking all nails for any early-stage lifting.

If you find minor lifting at an edge:
- **Do not pick at it** (the most common mistake — it pulls off layers of the nail plate, making it thinner over time)
- Apply a small amount of top coat or nail strengthener over the lifted edge as a temporary fix until your next appointment

If lifting covers more than ⅓ of the nail or you notice trapped debris underneath, book a fill or removal promptly — don't wait.

### Step 3: Full Hand Hydration

Gel protects the surface of your nail plate, but the nail itself and surrounding skin need more than cuticle oil alone.

- Once a week, use a rich hand cream (look for urea, hyaluronic acid, or shea butter) and do a 5-minute hand massage
- Focus on knuckles, sides of fingers, and the back of the hand

For intensive moisture during dry winter months: apply thick hand cream, put on cotton gloves, and sleep with them on. The overnight occlusion effect is hard to beat.

## Three Habits That Will Damage Gel Faster Than Anything

1. **Using nails as tools**: Prying open soda tabs, scraping labels, pulling out staples — the mechanical stress is far greater than it feels, and a single incident can cause a clean break or wide-area lifting

2. **Extended hot water exposure**: Dishwashing and hot baths cause gel to expand and soften. Use gloves for dishes and limit soaking time

3. **Peeling gel yourself**: This takes the top layer of your natural nail with it. Nails become progressively thinner with each peel, and eventually gel simply won't bond well at all

## Sample Weekly Schedule

| Timing | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Every morning and/or before bed | Cuticle oil application |
| Once weekly (weekends work well) | Exfoliate cuticles + inspect edges + deep hand moisturizing |
| Every 2–3 weeks | Fill appointment or full color change |

Follow this rhythm and your gel will still look full and intact at the three-week mark — consistently.
