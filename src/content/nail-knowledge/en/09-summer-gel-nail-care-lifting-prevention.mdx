## Introduction

Every summer, many people face the same frustration: "My gel nails just lifted and started peeling less than a week after getting them done." This isn't necessarily your fault, and it's not always the nail technician's fault either — heat, humidity, and sweat are gel nails' worst enemies in summer. Here are 5 essential tips to keep your gel manicure intact through the hottest months.

---

## 1. Pre-Service: Thorough Degreasing Is Everything

In summer, skin oil production increases 2–3 times compared to winter, and your nail plates are no exception. If the nail surface isn't properly degreased before application, the base coat simply won't bond tightly — and lifting is inevitable.

**The right approach:**
- Wipe the nail surface with a cotton pad soaked in **70%+ isopropyl alcohol** before applying base coat. Wait 30 seconds for it to fully evaporate.
- Don't touch the cleaned nail surface with your fingers — skin oils will transfer immediately.
- For those with naturally oily nails, ask your nail technician to apply a **specialized primer** after the alcohol prep for maximum adhesion.

---

## 2. Edge Sealing: Cap Every Single Layer

Lifting almost always starts at the **free edge** of the nail. Water, dish soap, and sweat only need a 0.5mm gap to seep in and gradually separate the entire gel from the nail.

**The right technique:**
- Cap the free edge on **every layer** — base coat, color coat, and top coat. No skipping.
- Lightly press the brush tip against the free edge to form a thin "edge seal."
- In summer, ask your nail technician to **double-cap the free edge on the top coat** for extra protection.

---

## 3. Summer-Specific Base Coats: Choose Strong-Adhesion Formulas

Standard base coats provide adequate adhesion in cooler weather, but summer heat can slightly soften gel products and reduce bonding strength. Consider upgrading to:

- **Acid Primer**: Ideal for oily nail plates or frequent lifters. It micro-etches the nail surface to improve adhesion. Use only under professional guidance — improper use can thin the nail plate.
- **Rubber Base**: Flexible and shock-absorbing, it resists chipping from everyday impacts and won't crack from the edges. Highly recommended as a summer base coat choice.

---

## 4. First 48 Hours After Service: Avoid Heat and Moisture

Freshly applied gel nails are at their most vulnerable in the first 48 hours. The bond between the base coat and nail plate hasn't fully set, making them susceptible to premature lifting.

**What to avoid in the first two days:**
- Extended soaking in hot springs, swimming pools, or bathtubs.
- Washing dishes or doing housework without rubber gloves.
- Prolonged direct sun exposure (UV heat can slightly soften the top coat).

---

## 5. Daily Maintenance: Cuticle Oil Is Your Best Friend

Many people think gel nails require no care after the appointment — but the opposite is true. Cuticle oil keeps the skin around the nail flexible and supple, reducing micro-tears at the gel edge caused by dry, stiff skin.

**Recommended summer care routine:**
- Apply cuticle oil morning and evening, focusing on the junction between the gel edge and the skin.
- After outdoor activities, reapply cuticle oil after washing your hands.
- If you notice the early signs of edge lifting, **don't pry it up with your fingers** — return to the salon for an edge repair immediately to prevent the whole nail from coming off.

---

## Summary

Summer gel nail lifting is caused by oil and moisture infiltration in 90% of cases. Thorough degreasing before application, capping every layer's edge, choosing the right base coat, and avoiding heat and humidity for the first 48 hours will help your gel manicure last three weeks or more — even through the hottest season. If lifting remains a persistent issue, discuss your nail type and product options with your nail technician.
