## The Essential Gear

### Ring Light / Softbox

Natural light is best, but for indoor shoots a ring light (10–18 inch) or mini softbox eliminates harsh shadows on curved nail surfaces. Position at 45° to the nail to show dimension and color depth.

### Smartphone Camera Settings

| Setting | Recommended Value |
|---------|------------------|
| Resolution | 1080p or 4K |
| Focus | Tap-to-focus on the nail (not background) |
| ISO | 100–400 (lower = less noise) |
| White Balance | Auto or Daylight |
| Grid Lines | Enable for rule-of-thirds |

### Tripod / Phone Mount

Prevents motion blur. Even a $12 mini tripod makes a significant difference for close-up nail shots.

## The 4 Essential Photo Angles

### 1. Flat Lay (Top Down)
Lay the hand flat on a clean surface, shoot from directly above. Best for: showing full nail designs, color accuracy, overall composition.

### 2. 45-Degree Angle
Most versatile angle. Shows nail shape, dimension, and depth simultaneously. Best for: gel overlay designs, gradient nails, Stiletto/almond shapes.

### 3. Close-Up Detail
Macro lens or portrait mode, focused on one nail. Shows: texture, chrome effects, nail art details, brush strokes.

### 4. Hand Model Shot
Full hand in frame, natural pose. Shows: how design looks in context, client-appeal, lifestyle context.

## Background and Composition Tips

**Do:**
- Use solid neutral backgrounds (white, black, marble texture, linen)
- Include a props context: nail tools, flowers, coffee — keeps the focus on nails
- Leave negative space for Instagram grid coherence
- Match background to nail color for color-story aesthetic

**Don't:**
- Busy patterns that compete with the nail design
- Cluttered workspaces
- Shooting on mirrors (causes unwanted reflections and doubling)

**Pro tip:** A white marble tile ($5 from hardware store) is the most versatile nail photography surface in 2026.

## Lighting: The #1 Factor

| Lighting Type | Result | Best For |
|--------------|--------|----------|
| Natural window light (north-facing) | Softest, most accurate color | White/cream/pastel gel |
| Ring light (direct) | Bright, even, slight shadow | Chrome/metallic nails |
| Softbox (45° side) | Professional dimension | Art nails, dark colors |
| LED panel (adjustable temp) | Full control | Consistent portfolio shoots |

**Critical:** Never use flash directly — it creates a blown-out white spot on the glossy nail surface. If shooting at an event with flash, bounce it off a white wall or ceiling.

## Color Accuracy: Match Screen to Reality

Gel colors photograph differently than they appear on-screen due to monitor calibration. To ensure color accuracy:
1. Edit under the same lighting you'll show the photo under
2. Compare edited photo to the actual gel bottle under natural light
3. Add a white balance card (available for $8 on Amazon) to your flat lay for consistent color grading

---

## How to Photograph Dark Gel Colors

Dark gels (black, deep purple, navy) are notoriously hard to photograph. Use:
- **Backlighting** — light coming from behind the hand to show depth
- **White surface underneath** — creates a clean backdrop that reflects light up onto the nail
- **Slightly overexpose** (+0.3 to +0.7 EV) — dark nails lose detail if shot at standard exposure
