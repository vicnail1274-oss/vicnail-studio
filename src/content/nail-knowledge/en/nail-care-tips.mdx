## Why Nail Care Matters

Healthy nails are the foundation of great nail art. Even the most stunning designs fall short on unhealthy nails. Brittle, peeling, or discolored nails are often signs of neglected daily care. Consistent nail maintenance helps gel polish adhere longer, colors look more vibrant, and reduces the risk of damage or allergic reactions from repeated treatments.

## 5 Golden Rules

### 1. Regular Trimming & Shaping

Use a nail file instead of clippers to avoid edge cracking or delamination. Aim to shape your nails every 1–2 weeks to keep edges clean and uniform.

**Key tips:**
- File in one direction only — back-and-forth sawing causes splitting
- Finish with a fine-grit buffer to smooth any rough edges
- Work with your natural nail shape rather than forcing a dramatic change

### 2. Daily Cuticle Oil Application

Cuticle oil is the cornerstone of nail care. It hydrates the skin around the nail, supports healthy nail growth, and keeps the edges of gel manicures looking neat and lifted.

**How to apply:**
- Apply morning and night to the base of each nail
- Gently massage for 30 seconds to boost absorption
- Bedtime application is most effective — pair it with hand cream for an overnight treatment

**Look for:** jojoba oil, argan oil, vitamin E, sunflower seed oil

### 3. Minimize Chemical and Water Exposure

Dish soap, bleach, household cleaners, and prolonged water contact are all enemies of healthy nails. They weaken the gel bond from the edges and thin out the natural nail plate over time.

**Protection habits:**
- Always wear rubber gloves when washing dishes or using cleaning agents
- Pat nails dry gently after bathing — avoid vigorous rubbing
- After swimming, rinse off chlorine residue and reapply cuticle oil

### 4. Support Nail Health Through Nutrition

Nail quality starts from within. Nutritional deficiencies can cause brittle nails, slow growth, vertical ridges, or white spots.

**Essential nutrients:**
- **Biotin (Vitamin B7):** Stimulates keratin production and strengthens the nail plate. Sources: eggs, almonds, oats
- **Zinc:** Supports cell regeneration and repair. Sources: pumpkin seeds, beef, legumes
- **Iron:** Iron deficiency can cause ridging and spoon-shaped nails. Sources: spinach, red meat, tofu
- **Protein:** Nails are made of keratin, a protein — inadequate intake directly affects nail thickness and growth

### 5. Give Your Nails a Break

Continuous gel manicures without downtime can weaken the natural nail. Between every 2–3 sets, schedule 1–2 gel-free weeks to let your nails breathe and recover.

**During the break:**
- Use a strengthening base coat with calcium or biotin
- Apply cuticle oil twice daily
- Avoid switching to other artificial nail systems (press-ons, acrylics) during recovery

## Common Nail Care Mistakes

| Wrong habit | Better alternative |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Biting nails or using them as tools | Keep a small scissor or letter opener nearby |
| Peeling off gel without soaking | Soak with remover and gently push off |
| Only using cuticle oil at the salon | Apply every single day at home |
| Using acetone remover daily | Switch to a low-acetone formula for regular use |

## The Bottom Line

Healthy nails don't come from a single salon visit — they're built through small daily habits. Remember the 5 golden rules: **trim, moisturize, protect, nourish, and rest**. Stick with them and you'll notice stronger nails, longer-lasting gel manicures, and a healthier overall canvas for every design.
