You just walked out of the salon with a stunning set of gel nail extensions — longer, perfectly shaped, and absolutely gorgeous. Now what?

Many people know to avoid soaking gel nails in water, but **gel nail extensions** actually require an extra level of care compared to regular gel polish. The added length creates a leverage effect: everyday actions that seem harmless (like opening a pull tab or peeling stickers) can snap an extension right off — or worse, damage your natural nail underneath.

This guide focuses primarily on **builder gel and BIAB-type extensions**. If you've just had your extensions done, here are 5 things you need to know.

---

## Extensions vs. Regular Gel Polish: What's Different?

| Aspect | Regular Gel Polish | Gel Nail Extensions |
|--------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Nail length | Natural nail length | Extended 5–15mm |
| Break risk | Low | Medium to High (leverage effect) |
| Infill frequency | Every 2–4 weeks | **Every 2–3 weeks** |
| Removal method | Acetone soak | Filing + soak (more involved) |
| Daily restrictions | Few | **More (requires more care)** |

The key issue with gel nail extensions is the **leverage effect**: the longer the extension, the more uneven the force distribution. What feels like a light tap to you can translate to significant stress at the nail bed junction.

## 5 Daily Care Rules for Gel Nail Extensions

### 1. Avoid Prolonged Water Exposure

Washing dishes, taking long baths, swimming — all of these accelerate lifting in gel extensions. Water seeps into the micro-gaps between the gel and your nail bed, weakening adhesion over time.

**What to do instead:**
- Wear rubber gloves when washing dishes or cleaning
- Keep baths or hot tub soaks under 15 minutes
- After washing hands, gently pat dry — don't shake your hands forcefully

### 2. Never Use Your Extensions as Tools

This is the #1 cause of extension breakage: using your nails to open soda cans, pry apart keyboards, or pick off stickers.

**Better alternatives:**
- Use the side of your finger or a card to open pull tabs
- Use a pen or card edge to lift stickers
- Type with your finger pads, not your nail tips (takes adjustment, but your extensions will last much longer)

### 3. Apply Cuticle Oil Every Day

Cuticle oil is your gel extensions' best friend. It moisturizes the skin around your nail bed, prevents hangnails, and keeps both the gel and natural nail flexible — reducing the risk of cracking and lifting.

**Best practices:**
- Apply around each nail base before bed
- Look for formulas with jojoba oil, almond oil, or vitamin E
- The first three days post-application are especially important

### 4. Be Mindful of How You Sleep and Apply Pressure

Many clients wake up to lifted extensions after unknowingly pressing their nails against a surface all night.

**Tips:**
- Avoid sleeping with your hand pressed under your face
- When carrying heavy bags, distribute weight across your palm and wrist — don't let your fingertips take the load
- Open doors and press elevator buttons with the side of your finger or knuckle

### 5. Gently Push Back Cuticles Regularly

After getting extensions, the cuticle area tends to produce more visible dead skin. If not maintained, it can make your nails look unkempt and trap dirt near the base.

**How to do it safely:**
1. After a shower (when cuticles are softened), use an orange wood stick to gently push back the skin
2. Avoid metal tools — they can damage the nail bed
3. Follow up with cuticle oil

## 3 Common Mistakes That Ruin Extensions

**❌ Mistake 1: Picking or Peeling a Lifted Extension**
When an extension starts to lift, the temptation to pick it off is real. Don't. Peeling it away tears off layers of your natural nail with it, causing thinning that makes future extensions lift even faster — a vicious cycle.

**✅ What to do instead:** Schedule a repair appointment as soon as possible. If the lifted edge is sharp, carefully trim the edge with nail scissors to prevent snagging — but don't pull.

---

**❌ Mistake 2: Skipping Your Infill Appointment**
"It hasn't broken yet, so I'll wait" — this logic leads to extensions with 6–7mm of visible natural nail growth at the base. Not only does it look obvious, but the leverage on the extension increases dramatically, making breakage far more likely.

**✅ What to do instead:** Book your infill (also called fill-in) at the **2–3 week mark**, even if everything looks fine.

---

**❌ Mistake 3: Using Nail Polish Remover on Gel Extensions**
Regular acetone-based nail polish remover will cloud the surface of your gel extensions, strip the shine, and can compromise the structural integrity of the gel.

**✅ What to do instead:** For daily cleaning, use a soft toothbrush with mild soap and water. For color changes, book a salon appointment.

---

## How to Know When It's Time for an Infill

Unlike regular gel manicures, gel extensions can't follow a "if it looks okay, wait longer" approach — because your natural nail is constantly growing. Each month, nails grow approximately 3–4mm, pushing the extension attachment point progressively toward the tip and increasing breakage risk.

**Recommended timing:**
- **2–3 weeks:** Infill appointment — fill in the base area, no need to remove the whole set
- **6–8 weeks:** Full removal and reapplication (when the shape has grown out significantly)

**Factors that affect timing:**
- How fast your nails grow naturally (faster in summer)
- Your occupation (hands-on work, cooking — more frequent infills needed)
- How well you follow the care rules above

## My Extension Broke — Emergency Steps

Extensions break at the worst moments. Here's how to handle it calmly:

**Step 1: Assess the damage**
- Just the tip chipped? → Trim the jagged edge with nail scissors to stop it from snagging further
- Broke close to the nail bed, with pain or bleeding? → Seek medical attention or go straight to the salon

**Step 2: Stabilize (if the extension is still partially attached)**
- Don't force it off
- Secure it temporarily with a piece of medical tape or clear tape
- Get to the salon as soon as possible

**Step 3: Go to the salon**
Tell your nail technician what happened — including what caused the break. This helps them determine whether it's a technique issue, a care issue, or a nail bed issue, and advise on repair vs. full reapplication.

## When Should You Completely Remove Your Extensions?

Extensions shouldn't go on forever. Here are signs it's time for a full removal:
- More than 6mm of natural nail growth at the base, causing the shape to look distorted
- Widespread lifting, yellowing, or a musty smell (possible fungal risk)
- Your natural nails feel noticeably thinner or more fragile than usual
- You're ready for a style change — like going shorter and lighter for summer
