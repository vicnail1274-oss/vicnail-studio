## What Is the Cuticle?

The cuticle is a thin layer of dead skin that seals the gap between your nail plate and the skin around it. Its job: protecting your nail matrix (where new nail cells grow) from bacteria and fungi. Picking or cutting cuticles aggressively is the single most common mistake that leads to infection and nail damage.

**Source:** American Academy of Dermatology (AAD), 2025 — https://www.aad.org/public/skin-hair-nails/nail-care/cuticles

---

## Why Cuticle Care Matters for Gel Nails

When you get gel nails, the cuticle must be properly pushed back and cleansed. If gel product touches the cuticle, it can cause lifting, allergic reactions, and moisture buildup that breeds bacteria.

A properly prepped cuticle:
- Creates a clean seal so gel doesn't peel
- Reduces risk of onycholysis (nail lifting from nail bed)
- Keeps the nail matrix healthy for strong regrowth

## Step-by-Step: Professional Cuticle Prep at Home

### Tools You Need

- Cuticle pusher (metal or orange wood stick)
- Cuticle remover gel (with potassium hydroxide or sodium hydroxide)
- Cuticle oil (jojoba-based is closest to natural sebum)
- Soft cotton pads or swabs
- Nail cleanser / isopropyl alcohol

### Step 1: Soften the Cuticle

Soak fingers in warm water for 3–5 minutes, or apply cuticle remover gel for 30–60 seconds. Never force the cuticle back dry.

### Step 2: Gently Push Back

Use a cuticle pusher at a 45° angle. Push from the free edge toward the nail fold gently. Don't dig into the nail plate or pierce the skin.

### Step 3: Remove Excess Skin

Dead cuticle skin that sits on the nail plate can be wiped away with a cotton swab. Do NOT use nippers on live cuticle tissue.

### Step 4: Apply Cuticle Oil

After every hand wash and before bed, apply 1 drop of jojoba cuticle oil per nail and massage in for 10 seconds. This keeps the nail plate moisturized and prevents hard, overgrown cuticles.

## How Often Should You Do Cuticle Care?

- **Daily:** Apply cuticle oil 2–3x per day
- **Every 1–2 weeks:** Push back and remove dead skin during a manicure
- **Every 4–6 weeks (gel nails):** Get cuticle care done professionally during gel fill appointment

---

## Signs of an Infected Cuticle

See a doctor if you notice:
- Redness spreading beyond the nail fold
- Pus or yellow discharge
- Throbbing pain lasting more than 2 days
- Nail separation from the nail bed

These symptoms suggest paronychia (cuticle infection) and require oral antibiotics.
