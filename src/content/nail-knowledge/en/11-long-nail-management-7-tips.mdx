Long gel nails turn heads — but they also need a little extra care in everyday life. With the right habits, you can enjoy gorgeous length without the constant frustration of chips and breaks.

---

## 1. Use Your Fingerpads, Not Your Fingertips

The number one cause of long nail breakage is using your fingertips to tap keyboards, press phone screens, or pry things open. Train yourself to use the **flat pad of your finger** for everyday tasks. Your nails are there to look beautiful — they don't need to do the heavy lifting.

---

## 2. Always Wear Gloves for Housework

Dish soap, cleaning sprays, and bleach are gel nail enemies. Prolonged contact weakens the gel bond and can make your natural nails brittle underneath. A pair of rubber gloves is the simplest, most effective protection you can add to your routine.

---

## 3. Apply Cuticle Oil Morning and Night

Cuticle oil does more than look nice — it penetrates the nail edge and seals tiny gaps that let moisture creep in and loosen the gel. Applying it twice a day (morning and before bed) keeps gel adhesion stronger for longer. A bedtime massage is especially effective since the oil has all night to absorb.

---

## 4. Never Use Your Nails as Tools

Ripping tape, popping phone cases, pulling ring pulls — these actions put sudden lateral stress on long nails and are a leading cause of breaks. Keep a small letter opener, coin, or pen cap handy for these tasks. Small habit, big protection.

---

## 5. Book a Fill Every 2 Weeks Once Length Exceeds 1cm

The longer your extension, the more leverage force acts on the nail with every bump or impact. When your nail length extends more than 1cm past your fingertip, scheduling a maintenance fill every two weeks is worth it. Your technician can spot early lifting and fix it before a minor issue turns into a break.

---

## 6. Keep a Nail File in Your Bag

If a small chip happens while you're out, don't pull at it — that almost always makes the damage worse. A fine-grit nail file lets you smooth the jagged edge on the spot, preventing it from catching on fabric. Apply a coat of clear polish when you get home, then get a proper repair within the week.

---

## 7. Nourish Before Bed: Hand Cream + Cuticle Oil

Long nails combined with dry conditions are a recipe for breakage. After washing your hands at night, apply a generous layer of hand cream and massage it in, then finish with cuticle oil. The overnight absorption keeps nails flexible and resilient — and your skin soft too.
