## How AR Nail Try-On Technology Works

Modern AR nail try-on tools operate across three technical layers:

### 1. Hand Pose Estimation

Using the phone's camera, the system captures hand joint positions and identifies the precise location and angle of each nail area. Leading tools use MediaPipe or proprietary computer vision models to accurately locate all ten nails in real-time video.

### 2. Skin Tone Analysis and Color Matching

Once the hand is mapped, the AI analyzes the skin tone (cool / warm / neutral) and applies color theory principles to automatically recommend the most flattering gel nail color families:

- **Cool undertones** (pink or blue-leaning): Cool grays, rose tones, berry shades
- **Warm undertones** (yellow or orange-leaning): Earth tones, coral orange, cream whites
- **Neutral undertones**: Almost all palettes work; nude pinks and mint greens are particularly vibrant

### 3. Real-Time Rendering

After selecting a color, the AI renders it live onto the camera view, simulating what the gel nail would actually look like — including the finish (glossy vs. matte) and transparency level.

## Integrating AR Try-On into Your Workflow

**Step 1: Pre-Color Selection (After Booking Confirmation)**

Add one line to your booking confirmation message:
"Click here to use AR try-on and find your favorite style before your appointment — so we can start right away!"

This simple step means clients arrive with a decision made, eliminating 10–15 minutes of in-person color selection.

**Step 2: Set Up Your Custom Style Menu**

In the try-on platform's backend, configure only the colors and styles you actually have in stock. Avoid clients falling in love with a color you can't deliver.

**Step 3: Use Try-On Screenshots as Design Confirmation**

Ask clients to send you their try-on screenshot as the design brief. This screenshot becomes a pre-service design agreement — significantly reducing "I don't like it" disputes after completion.
