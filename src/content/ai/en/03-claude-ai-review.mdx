## Three Months With Claude AI — Here's My Honest Take

I'm Vic. I run a nail studio in Taipei.

Three months ago, I had zero interest in AI tools. A friend kept pushing them on me and I thought: "I'm not an engineer, what does any of this have to do with me?"

Now I use it every single day.

This isn't a technical review. I can't tell you anything about model architecture or API integration. I'm just a solo studio owner who juggles bookings, LINE messages, Instagram, and monthly accounting — and I'm going to tell you what three months with Claude AI has actually looked like in my day-to-day work.

---

## First Impression: I Was Genuinely Surprised

The very first time I opened Claude, I pasted in a client's LINE conversation and asked it: "What does this client want, and how should I reply?"

It didn't just identify what kind of nail style she was asking about. It wrote me a response that felt natural and warm — the same tone I actually use with clients. Not too formal, not too casual.

I expected something stiff and templated. What I got was something I could copy and paste directly.

What I figured out later: if I give Claude context upfront — like "my studio has a clean, elevated Japanese aesthetic, my clients are mostly professional women in their late 20s to 40s" — it holds onto that and all its responses stay consistent with my brand voice.

---

## The 3 Things I Use It For Every Day

### 1. Drafting LINE Replies (Copy-Paste Ready)

This is by far my most frequent use.

You can't imagine the volume of LINE messages a nail studio gets. "How much is this style?" "Do you have any openings?" "My nails are really short, can I still get extensions?" Every day, these alone can eat an hour or more.

My current workflow: paste the client's message into Claude, add "reply in natural Traditional Chinese, friendly but professional." What comes back is usable as-is about 80% of the time. The other 20% needs a small tweak.

That saved time effectively adds one bookable client slot to my day.

### 2. Writing Instagram Captions (Hashtags Included)

I knew IG mattered but I was never good at writing copy. My photos were fine; the captions were always just "Today's work 🌸" — and the engagement reflected it.

I started describing my work to Claude instead: "French white gel with shell accents, client wanted a beach wedding vibe." Then asked for an IG caption with a sense of atmosphere plus 20 hashtags relevant to the Taiwanese nail market.

The results are ready to post. The copy has visual texture and feels lived-in — better than anything I was writing myself.

My posting frequency went from once a week to three times a week, because the "writing the caption" barrier essentially disappeared.

### 3. Monthly Financial Analysis (Paste Your Records, Get Insight)

This one I didn't see coming.

My bookkeeping was scattered — LINE Pay records here, a handwritten notebook there. Month-end reconciliation used to be miserable.

One month I just dumped everything — an entire month of transaction records — into Claude and asked: "Calculate total revenue, tell me which services are most profitable, what time slots are most in demand, and flag anything I should consider adjusting."

The analysis was clear and logical. It also proactively noted that a few of my service prices seemed low relative to their time cost and suggested I consider adjusting them.

## The Honest Downsides

Not everything is perfect, and I'd rather be straight with you.

**The biggest issue: sometimes the tone is too formal.**

Especially for LINE replies, the output occasionally sounds like a public announcement. I fixed this by adding to my instructions: "Write like you're talking to a friend — casual but professional." That mostly solves it.

The other friction point: every new conversation starts fresh. You have to re-explain your studio context each time. My solution: I keep a "studio background" note saved on my phone. Takes about 10 seconds to paste at the start of a new session.

## Is It Worth It for a Small Studio?

**Yes — but you need to invest a little time in setup.**

A lot of people open an AI tool expecting it to work perfectly on the first try. They use it twice, feel underwhelmed, and stop. I almost did the same.

The key: the more specific context and instructions you give it, the better the output. Spend a couple of weeks testing different prompts and figuring out what works for your studio. After that, it gets faster every time.

For me: Claude AI freed up time, eliminated caption anxiety, and made my finances more readable. Those are tangible returns.

If you're a small business owner who hasn't tried it yet, my advice is simple: **start with the free version.** That's enough to feel what it can do before deciding whether to upgrade.
