## Why "Making Recommendations" Is One of the Hardest Skills in Nail

Most nail technicians are highly skilled at their craft, but the consultation stage trips many of them up.

> "What style would you like?"
> "I don't know — just surprise me."

This exchange is incredibly common, and deceptively difficult. When a client says "anything," she doesn't actually mean anything. She has preferences — she just can't articulate them.

In 2026, AI tools give you a systematic way to uncover what a client actually wants and deliver a recommendation that feels personal, not random.

---

## A Three-Step AI-Assisted Consultation Process

### Step 1: Ask the Right Questions

The quality of your recommendation depends entirely on the quality of your intake questions. Design a simple five-question intake form for clients to fill out when booking or on arrival:

1. **What's the main purpose of this set?** (Daily wear, date night, wedding, photoshoot)
2. **Preferred color family?** (Warm / cool / neutral / unsure)
3. **Style preference?** (Minimalist / cute / bold / natural / edgy)
4. **Nail length preference?** (Short / medium / long)
5. **Any elements you dislike?** (Glitter, 3D embellishments, nail art, etc.)

This can be a Google Form or LINE survey linked in your booking confirmation — clients fill it out before they even arrive.

### Step 2: Use AI to Analyze and Generate Recommendations

Paste the client's answers into ChatGPT using this prompt framework:

> **"I'm a nail technician in Taiwan. Client preferences: purpose = daily wear + date nights, color preference = warm tones, style = minimalist and elegant, nail length = medium, dislikes glitter. Please suggest 3 gel nail design directions, each with color descriptions and design notes."**

ChatGPT will return structured options like:

**Option A: Warm Nude Minimalist**
- Color: Warm nude beige
- Design: 4 fingers solid + 1 finger delicate French gel line
- Ideal for: Daily office wear, casual dates

**Option B: Terracotta Statement**
- Color: Deep terracotta red
- Design: Solid gel, high-gloss finish
- Ideal for: Clients who want personality without going over the top

**Option C: Caramel Gradient**
- Color: Caramel brown gradient
- Design: Ombre gel with subtle gold foil at tips
- Ideal for: Date nights, special occasions

### Step 3: Visualize the Recommendations

Text recommendations land harder with visual support.

- **Midjourney**: Input each option's description to generate reference images
- **Pinterest AI search**: Search keywords to find real-world reference photos
- **Your own portfolio**: Tag and organize your past work so you can find similar styles instantly

Present the images alongside the recommendation rationale. Client satisfaction — and conversion — will climb noticeably.

## Advanced: Build a Personal Style Profile for Each Client

After a few visits, you'll notice every client has consistent preferences. Create a "Client Style Profile" in Notion or Google Sheets:

| Field | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Style keywords | Minimalist, warm tones, understated |
| Recurring colors | Nude, earth tones |
| Avoid | Glitter, 3D embellishments |
| Preferred gel brands | OPI, CND |
| Last set | March 2026 — nude French gel |

Before each appointment, ask Notion AI to summarize: "What did this client get last time, and what might work well now?" You have a personalized starting point without mental effort.

## FAQ: Will AI Recommendations Feel Generic?

Not if your inputs are specific.

AI recommendations are a starting point, not a finished product. Your aesthetic judgment and your knowledge of this particular client are what make the final recommendation feel personal. Use AI to organize information and surface options — then apply your expertise to shape the final choice together with your client.

## Closing Thought

Recommendations are fundamentally about understanding a client's needs and translating them into a concrete design direction. AI accelerates and structures that translation.

Try it today: run your next client through five intake questions, feed the answers to ChatGPT, and see what it suggests. You might discover options you wouldn't have thought to propose yourself.
